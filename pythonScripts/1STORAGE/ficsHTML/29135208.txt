There is little love lost between the man and his fellow Peak Lords.</p><p class="p1">The man had never even <em>bonded</em> with the others of Cang Qiong Mountain sect. Their delegation smells united by verdant green scent that marked Yue Qingyuan.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu smells of nothing at all.</p><p class="p1">He looks so different after a month within the Water Prison. Those elaborately robes he had always favored were replaced by a single, simple white robe. His hair was messy, half-falling out of its ribbon.</p><p class="p1">Luo Binghe stares at him, not willing to miss any moment of his downfall. Beside him, the Little Palace Mistress croons and attempts to comfort him, holding one of his hands between her own, rubbing her scent and the scent of Huan Hua Palace into his skin.</p><p class="p1">The old Palace Master smiles pleasantly as Shen Qingqiu's crimes are read out.</p><p class="p1">The murder of the kindly Qiu family that had taken him in.</p><p class="p1">The attempted murder of his shidi.</p><p class="p1">Child abuse, the attempted murder of one of the disciples in his care.</p><p class="p1">Consorting with demons.</p><p class="p1">There were enough crimes to lock Shen Qingqiu within the Water Prison for the rest of his immortal life. And then no one would be able to stop Luo Binghe from getting his vengeance--as much as it would take to sate the violence within his heart.</p><p class="p1">The Bai Zhan War God stands up. "Retract the charge for attempting to kill me," he says stiffly.</p><p class="p1">"Immortal Master Liu," the Old Palace Master chides. "There is no reason to protest. We have all heard Shen Qingqiu say that he would kill you one day. It was perhaps a stroke of luck that you survived."</p><p class="p1">Liu Qingge does not sit. "Retract the charge."</p><p class="p1">Yue Qingyuan does not hush him but neither does he order his shidi to continue.</p><p class="p1">Cang Qiong Mountain sect is less structured than Luo Binghe had ever realized. To allow Shen Qingqiu, the second in terms of authority and a <em>beta</em>, ignore Yue Qingyuan's place in the hierarchy had been distasteful. To let another shidi and an <em>alpha</em> at that buck Yue Qingyuan's dominance, then, well, it seemed that Yue Qingyuan's authority was slipping.</p><p class="p1">The representatives of the sects murmur to one another and finally that charge is dropped.</p><p class="p1">But what is one charge against so many?</p><p class="p1">Even Shen Qingqiu realizes this surely. He stares dead ahead while seated on the platform, his hands bound behind his back. Even now, Shen Qingqiu pretended to be a graceful, distant Immortal--one untouched by mortal vice.</p><p class="p1"><em>Ha</em>.</p><p class="p1">Soon enough his Shizun's rotten insides would be known to all.</p><p class="p1">The Old Palace Master stands up. He smells of resin and of wood, he smells of pride and satisfaction. “This master has brought a special artifact for the trial," he says with a smile.</p><p class="p1">A disciple in Hua Huan Palace robes offers up a tiny wooden box. The Old Palace Master opens it, revealing a little night pearl.</p><p class="p1">“This is a Memory Pearl,” the Old Palace Master says. “This old master has searched long for such an artifact, so that we might bear witness to the atrocities we have allowed to linger for so long.”</p><p class="p1">There is a flutter of protest from Yue Qingyuan, but the Old Palace Master pays no mind to it.</p><p class="p1">He is the one to press the Memory Pearl against Shen Qingqiu’s forehead, as the man lets out small, pained whines and whimpers, noises that echo within the silence of the room.</p><p class="p1">How Luo Binghe had wanted to be the one to force those sounds out of Shen Qingqiu.</p><p class="p1">The Little Palace Mistress’s grip on him tightens, her fingers intertwining with his own.</p><p class="p1">The Old Palace Master steps back. He lets go of the Memory Pearl, but it remains floating at the center of the room, level with Shen Qingqiu’s forehead. The Memory Pearl grows brighter and brighter, until the room fades away.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Xiao Jiu is sold to the Qiu family.</p><p class="p1">“Come back and rescue me, okay, Qi-ge?” he says to no one.</p><p class="p1">Qi-ge had gone away hours ago, promising to become a cultivator strong enough to free Xiao Jiu from the Qi Manor.</p><p class="p1">
  <em>“This isn’t right,” one cultivator says to another. “Where is Shen Qingqiu?”</em>
</p><p class="p1">
  <em>The other shushes him. “Can’t you see what’s before your eyes?”</em>
</p><p class="p1">The days are long. Menial labor is difficult when you are a child and your body is weakened by lack of food.</p><p class="p1">The young master of the house beats him.</p><p class="p1">The young mistress doesn’t know.</p><p class="p1">Their father does not care.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Two children sit on the streets. They are dressed in little more than rags, clothing thin and drab, not warm enough for the weather.</p><p class="p1">“Qi-ge,” says one child, “you shouldn’t have given your food to the others. They gotta care for themselves.”</p><p class="p1">The other boy, this Qi-ge, smiles placidly and pats the boy’s head. “It’ll be okay, Xiao Jiu. I’m not hungry.”</p><p class="p1">Xiao Jiu snorts. Then, looking this way and that, he reaches into his sleeve and pulls out a half-eaten bun. It’s long gone cold. He hands it over to the older boy. “Eat,” he says.</p><p class="p1">This is a dream. A sliver of an even older memory.</p><p class="p1">This Qi-ge’s face is blurry and indistinct. He smells of nothing.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">One autumn day, Xiao Jiu wakes feverish.</p><p class="p1">He smells of milk candies. Something sweet. Subtle. So easy to miss.</p><p class="p1">Xiao Jiu goes about his day. Cleaning. Washing the floors on hands and knees, hands red and chapped and hurting. All of him aches.</p><p class="p1">The young master is the first to notice, letting out a bark of later. “So you’re an omega after all?”</p><p class="p1">
  <em>“No, that isn’t right.”</em>
</p><p class="p1">
  <em>“Shen Qingqiu is a beta. Something is wrong with the Memory Pearl.”</em>
</p><p class="p1">The young master hauls Xiao Jiu up by the collar of his robe and takes him to a confinement room.</p><p class="p1">There is a breeding bench at the center of the room and little else.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">The breeding bench.</p><p class="p1">The <em>breeding</em> bench.</p><p class="p1">These days are a blur of colors, shapes, and smells.</p><p class="p1">When alone, the little Xiao Jiu purrs to himself, as though trying to provide comfort. It does not work.</p><p class="p1">The room fills with the stench of rotten fruits, of milk gone sour.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">His days are filled with work.</p><p class="p1">His nights are more of the same. An omega’s body might be treasured, but it is not cared for.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Years pass.</p><p class="p1">And slowly, something within Xiao Jiu breaks. There is no light within his eyes.</p><p class="p1">He is much like a ghost around the Qiu manor.</p><p class="p1">“Xiao Jiu, Xiao Jiu!” the mistress of the house says. She is smiling brightly, not noticing how the child slave limps. “Come with me to the market! You can carry my things!”</p><p class="p1">This is allowed easily enough. The Qiu master and the young master will allow Qiu Haitang anything—so long as she never truly leaves their home.</p><p class="p1">Qiu Haitang strolls through the markets, buying all sorts of baubles, each more and more precious than the last. Hair pins. Brightly colored ribbons. Pretty fans.</p><p class="p1">Eventually, Qiu Haitang stops before a perfumer, buying precious vials of oils and perfumes. “These are for you Xiao Jiu,” she says cheerfully, before leaning in close. “Omegas are supposed to smell pretty.”</p><p class="p1">Xiao Jiu blinks dumbly at the very expensive things she buys. “Yes, Miss Haitang…”</p><p class="p1">None of the gifts Qiu Haitang gives to the omega stay with him long.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">“Qi-ge,” Shen Jiu says pitifully, curled up in his excuse of a nest—a single, ragged blanket against the cold floor of the shed. “I don’t know how much longer I can wait.”</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">The Qiu master dies. Of natural causes. Unfortunately.</p><p class="p1">The young master gains all of his father’s titles. The young master grows more and more bold.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">The next time Shen Jiu accompanies Qiu Haitang to the marketplace, he sees a lone cultivator.</p><p class="p1">This cultivator tells him that he has promise, that he too might be a strong cultivator.</p><p class="p1">Shen Jiu brings this knowledge to Qiu Jianluo, asking for permission to go and cultivate. He purrs then, more of that quiet, self-soothing purring that doesn’t seem to truly work.</p><p class="p1">“Xiao Jiu?” Qiu Haitang says, coming across his prone body. “What’s wrong?”</p><p class="p1">Shen Jiu cannot answer, save for the insufferable purr that escape his throat. There is blood dripping from between his legs.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">It is only because of Qiu Haitang’s panic that a healer is brought to the Qiu manor.</p><p class="p1">“A miscarriage,” the healer says, wiping his hands clean.</p><p class="p1">And he purrs and purrs and purrs, blinking away the tears that spring to his eyes.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">The healer had said that the omega would need at least a month to recover, if not longer, given how weak the body was.</p><p class="p1">Shen Jiu is given a week’s worth of rest before he has to return to responsibilities.</p><p class="p1">One of the kitchen women comes to care for him whenever she can. It does very little.</p><p class="p1">“You know,” the kitchen woman says. “Omegas are precious and treated like treasures, but only when they’re born into good families. You stink of distressed omega and no one wants to be around that.”</p><p class="p1">Shen Jiu lowers his head. The liquid within the little cup is dark and smells obnoxiously bitter. “Yes, shifu,” he says in a small voice before swallowing it down.</p><p class="p1">“I’ll show you what you need to make yourself that tonic, but you better remember to drink it once a month so you don’t insult everyone’s noses.”</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Wu Yanzi is dishonorable.</p><p class="p1">He teaches slaughter, arson, robbery, and all sorts of dirty tricks.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Wu Yanzi binds him with an Immortal Binding Cable and gifts him to some random alpha for a night, all for a small purse of coin.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Shen Jiu drinks the tonic religiously and begins taking suppressants, buying them from the red light districts. He eats the suppressants like candy and he can never have enough.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Wu Yanzi is killed by Shen Jiu.</p><p class="p1">“How could you?” Shen Jiu says. “Did you not want me, Qi-ge? Did you rejoice when you left me behind? Why did you break your promise?”</p><p class="p1">“Qi-ge is so sorry, Xiao Jiu.”</p><p class="p1">And the then disciple Yue Qingyuan spirits him away to Cang Qiong Mountain sect.</p><p class="p1">
  <em>“Surely… this is enough—”</em>
</p><p class="p1">
  <em>“Palace Master, I insist—”</em>
</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Shen Jiu sleeps in the woodshed.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Years pass by in a series of blinks.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Luo Binghe is taken in as a disciple, eyes bright and cheerful. He recites his reason for joining Cang Qiong Mountain sect as Shen Qingqiu dips a finger in a vial of medicine.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu stirs his tea with this finger before pouring it over Luo Binghe’s face.</p><p class="p1">The little injuries he bore sealed shut as the smile disappeared from his face.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu is harsh. Stern. Unyielding. Openly jealous of the children he calls disciples. Spiteful of those alphas of Cang Qiong Mountain sect—the disciples and masters alike.</p><p class="p1">“The boy will likely present as an omega with that pretty face of his,” he says idly when he visits the brothel. “It would be kinder if he died before then.”</p><p class="p1">Cheng Qi clucks her tongue but does not disagree.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">In the Lingxi Caves, there is a one-sided battle.</p><p class="p1">The Bai Zhan War God is experiencing a qi deviation. He throws himself at Shen Qingqiu, swinging Cheng Luan wildly this way and that. He is screaming, he will not stop screaming.</p><p class="p1">“You fool!” Shen Qingqiu shouts, bleeding from his mouth. “Stop this right now! You’ll kill us both!”</p><p class="p1">But Liu Qingge is beyond comprehension. He throws himself after Shen Qingqiu.</p><p class="p1">And then something breaks.</p><p class="p1">The scent of distressed omega is thick in the cave—rotten fruit, having long lost even a hint of sweetness.</p><p class="p1">Liu Qingge blinks. There is a sliver of recognition in his eyes. “Omega…?” he says, voice thick.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu does not get up where he lies, curled on the ground, hands wrapped around his throat, covering his scent glands, however futilely. He is purring once again, that damned reflex that never truly could help to soothe himself.</p><p class="p1">Liu Qingge sits down next to him, suppressing his own scent. He creeps forwards, rubbing his cheek against Shen Qingqiu’s wrist, rubbing Shen Qingqiu’s scent against himself.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu stiffens. Where before he had been on the verge of a qi deviation, now he falls into it.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Yue Qingyuan finds them, both bloody and bruised, their scents mysteriously suppressed.</p><p class="p1">It’s a miracle that they both still live.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Luo Binghe sleeps in the woodshed.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">At the Immortal Alliance Conference, Shen Qingqiu stares at the demon sigil burning across Luo Binghe’s head.</p><p class="p1">Without a word, he pushes the boy down, into the Endless Abyss.</p><p class="p1">
  <em>“Luo Binghe is a demon?”</em>
</p><p class="p1">
  <em>“Stop the Memory Pearl! We must extinguish the demon amongst us!”</em>
</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">But the Memory Pearl continues to show all of Shen Qingqiu’s secrets.</p><p class="p1">And how very many there are.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu never goes to the Peak Lords’ nest on Cang Qiong Mountain sect.</p><p class="p1">Instead, he goes down the mountain, to the Warm Red Pavilion, where he is welcomed into the brothel ladies’ nest.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">And it goes on and on and on until—</p><p class="p1">The Water Prison.</p><p class="p1">Luo Binghe stands before his Shizun and forces him to ingest his Heavenly Demon blood.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu might writhe in pain, but he never gives the satisfaction of letting out a sound or a scent.</p><p class="p1">Luo Binghe leaves.</p><p class="p1">Time passes.</p><p class="p1">Yue Qingyuan enters the room.</p><p class="p1">“…Shidi. It has come to all this already and you still won’t reflect on your own faults? Not even a little?”</p><p class="p1">Yue Qingyuan leaves and with it the Water Prison is dark again.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu purrs quietly to himself.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">The Memory Pearl falls and shatters against the ground.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu is still, his eyes red-rimmed. The room smells of rotten fruit, of sour milk, of an omega in serious and sustained distress. His breathing is labored, his body shaking within the bounds of the Immortal Binding Cable.</p><p class="p1">Liu Qingge is the first to move, unsheathing Cheng Luan and stepping between Shen Qingqiu and Luo Binghe.</p><p class="p1">“Impudent disciple,” is what Liu Qingge says first. Then he corrects himself. “<em>Demon.</em>”</p><p class="p1">Luo Binghe blinks slowly. Even the Little Palace Mistress has abandoned him.</p><p class="p1">Everyone knows what he is now. So there is no reason to hide.</p><p class="p1">Mu Qingfang dashes to Shen Qingqiu's side, freeing him from the Immortal Binding Cable, just as Luo Binghe allows his demon sigil to burn across his forehead.</p><p class="p1">Luo Binghe smiles. “Hello, shishu.”</p><p class="p1">His blood is still within Shen Qingqiu. He could—</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu attempts to shatter his core, with the whole room as witness.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">“Did you hear? Did you hear? Cang Qiong Mountain sect no longer has twelve Peak Lords, but eleven!”</p><p class="p1">“Ah! Was Peak Lord Shen found to be guilty?”</p><p class="p1">“No, no! That’s the thing.” Here, the village gossipmonger leans in closer to whisper. “Peak Lord Shen was an omega slave and his past was viewed by just about everyone in the cultivation world.”</p><p class="p1">A gasp. “Oh, how cruel. His mother didn’t kill him before it came to that?”</p><p class="p1">“Guess not. Seems like Peak Lord Shen is hiding from his pack now. The cultivation world is in an uproar!”</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">The Peak Lords gather together. Shen Qingqiu’s usual seat to the right of Yue Qingyuan is empty.</p><p class="p1">Mu Qingfang cannot help but look at it.</p><p class="p1">So much could have been done before it reached this point. But they hadn’t. They didn’t.</p><p class="p1">No one had protected Shen Qingqiu from the Water Prison. They had all been more than willing to give him up.</p><p class="p1">“We need to find him,” Mu Qingfang says quietly, echoing what everyone else must be thinking. “He wouldn’t let me see to his injuries.”</p><p class="p1">Qi Qingqi snorts. “Of course not. You’re an alpha and a man at that.” She sighs and looks at the nervous Shang Qinghua, the only other omega among them. “Do you think Shang-shidi could be trained in the arts of healing enough to see it Shen-shixiong once he’s found?”</p><p class="p1">“But I’m still a man,” Shang Qinghua squeaks out.</p><p class="p1">Yue Qingyuan is silent throughout the meeting. For once, the smile on his face has fallen. He looks <em>worried.</em></p><p class="p1">“We need to find him first,” Mu Qingfang asks. “And… this shidi is afraid that we might have to learn how to earn his trust.”</p><p class="p1">The Peak Lords’ low spirits only continue to plummet after he says this, but it is the truth. They had decades and yet they had never truly bonded with one of their own. And that lack of trust and affection led to <em>this.</em></p><p class="p1">People are laughing at Cang Qiong Mountain sect’s incompetence. How many years had passed. Even the last generation of Peak Lords had failed.</p><p class="p1">Shen Qingqiu paid the price.</p><p class="p1">“I,” says Yue Qingyuan, speaking for the first time this meeting. “I believe I know where he would be.”</p><p class="p1">But, before Yue Qingyuan went to attempt to meet with their lost martial brother, he does something unexpected. He destroys each and every breeding bench that belonged to Cang Qiong Mountain sect.</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">*</p><p class="p1">*</p>
