    <h2 class="title heading">
      Through A Glass Darkly
    </h2>
    <h3 class="byline heading">
      <a rel="author" href="/users/blondsak/pseuds/blondsak">blondsak</a>, <a rel="author" href="/users/seekrest/pseuds/seekrest">seekrest</a>
    </h3>


        <div class="summary module" role="complementary">
          <h3 class="heading">Summary:</h3>
            <blockquote class="userstuff">
              <p>“So am I dead? In this universe, I mean.”</p><p>Tony glances up sharply. “What? No.”</p><p>Peter nods, biting the inside of his cheek as he tries to calm his quaking nerves. “Okay… so if I’m not dead, then where am I? And why are you all treating me like I’m some sort of criminal?”</p><p>“Because here Spider-Man is a criminal,” Tony replies, unflinching. “And not just any common thief. A murderer.”</p><p>Peter barks out a laugh, the corner of his lip turning up. “Yeah, right.” But the smile falters when Tony’s expression doesn’t change. “No way, no <i>way</i> would I ever–”</p><p>“Oh, there’s a way, kid. Believe me,” Tony interjects. “The other you, <i>my</i> Peter? He bought himself a one-way ticket to the Raft. Been there for months.”</p><p>“No way,” Peter repeats with a croak. He looks down at his cuffed hands, feeling numb. “It’s a mistake. It has to be.”</p><p>Tony huffs out a humorless guffaw, and Peter lifts his head, wary but curious.</p><p>“Funny,” Tony says, deadly serious, “that’s what he said, too.”</p>
            </blockquote>

      <a href="/works/29252028/chapters/71829486">Chapter 1</a>

    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>“Peter, you’re gonna be late!”</p><p>“I’m coming,” Peter calls out, shoving his head through the hole of his sweater before pausing— glancing around his room.</p><p><em>What am I forgetting? What am I forgetting? </em>he muses to himself, the lightbulb going off as he grabs his backpack.</p><p>“May!” Peter yells, rifling through the various sheets of loose leaf paper, “Do you see my chem homework anywhere?”</p><p>“It’s on the counter!” May yells back, Peter stopping for a second before the memory of leaving it there hits— slinging his backpack over his shoulder as he walks out of his bedroom.</p><p>“If you kept everything in the trapper keeper I got you, you wouldn’t lose your homework,” May teases, Peter making a face as he walks over to the kitchen counter and grabs the missing homework.</p><p>“No one calls them that anymore,” Peter says, May laughing as she throws him a banana. Peter catches it with ease, grinning at her as he shoves it and the homework into his backpack. </p><p>“I’m surprised they give you paper homework still to begin with,” May says, waving her hand around as she leans against the counter and takes a sip of her coffee. Peter peruses the bowl of specially-made granola bars Tony recently sent over as she continues, “You’d think everything would be terabytes or digits or WIFI signals.”</p><p>“That’s not how any of that works,” Peter says with a laugh, going for the peanut butter and strawberry jam flavor he has as he rips it open and takes a bite, humming in contentment as May laughs.</p><p>“What won’t work is you being late for the third time in a row this week. You and that Johnny Storm stay out way too late.”</p><p>“No we don’t,” Peter says with his mouth full, May raising an eyebrow as Peter quickly chews then swallows his food. “Besides, Johnny doesn’t have a curfew.”</p><p>“Johnny <em> also </em> doesn’t have an 8am home room. Something <em> you </em> will be late for if you don’t skedaddle,” May says with a fond smile, Peter smiling again as he walks over to her.</p><p>She gives him a quick squeeze, Peter kissing her on the cheek before May sends him off on his way.</p><p>“Don’t forget to call Tony and tell him about AcaDec this weekend.”</p><p>“I won’t!” Peter calls out as he walks over the door, shoving another bite of the granola bar into his face and closing the door behind him.<br/><br/></p><hr/><p> </p><p>“Dude, you gotta invite Johnny to join our Beast Player team,” Ned whispers, Peter glaring at him before quickly glancing around the busy Midtown cafeteria. He shouldn’t be surprised that no one is looking in their direction but it’s still a concern all the same, Peter looking back at Ned as he says, “Dude.”</p><p>“Literally no one would guess what Johnny you’re talking about,” MJ deadpans, a credit to how long he’s known her and how good of friends they are that Peter can hear the hint of teasing in it. He glances over the table to see her head in a book, just barely hiding a smile that makes Peter’s stomach turn into knots as she continues, “You’re the literal worst person to keep a secret, you know that right?”</p><p>“What secret?” another voice says, MJ looking up and actually smiling as she puts her book down just as Gwen Stacy puts her tray down, Peter feeling like a deer in headlights as yet another voice chimes in, sitting next to Peter.</p><p>“The secret of Pete’s massive crush on MJ? Not much of a secret, if you ask me,” Harry Obsorn says with a wink, Peter feeling as if he could literally die on the spot as he coughs. </p><p>He tacitly avoids MJ’s eyes, forcing a smile and looking at Harry as he says, “Hey man, I thought your dad was taking you out of town.”</p><p>Harry rolls his eyes, shrugging as he rests his folded arms over the lunch table. “Put my foot down and said that if he’s gonna try and enroll me in a regular school to be ‘one with the people’,” he says, pantomiming his fingers, “then maybe I should <em> actually </em>attend school like one of the people.”</p><p>“Trip got cancelled?” Gwen asks, Harry scoffing as he leans back.</p><p>“Damn Stacy, why you gotta call me out like that?” Harry replies, the two of them devolving in the kind of banter that Peter’s gotten used to since they transferred in at the beginning of the school year.</p><p>He finally looks back to MJ who chews her lip, looking at him with a smirk on her face before looking elsewhere. Ned not so subtly nudges him in the side, the imploring look on his face yet another reminder that if his crush is that obvious to Harry and Gwen-- relative newcomers to Midtown-- that it’s probably obvious as hell to anyone else that’s known them for any length of time. </p><p>The rest of lunch passes comfortably, the bell bringing about another good-natured complaint from Harry about their time not being their own as they clear the table.</p><p>He’s not surprised to find himself walking in-step with MJ as they move to throw away their trash, Ned winking at Peter as he runs to catch up with Harry and Gwen as MJ clears her throat.</p><p>“So.”</p><p>“So,” Peter says, feeling his cheeks flush, “That was awkward.”</p><p>“Yeah,” MJ says with a laugh, throwing her recycling away and the rest of her trash in the correct bins, “But uh, he’s right you know.”</p><p>Peter freezes, forcing himself to look at her as MJ looks back at him awkwardly, only to smile a bit more genuinely as she says, “It’s not really that much of a secret.”</p><p>Peter clears his throat, going to say something when the next warning bell rings. </p><p>“I gotta get to Calc,” MJ says, pointing her thumb in the opposite direction. </p><p>“Yeah, yeah I got uh, AP Lit.” </p><p>“Nice.”</p><p>“Yeah,” Peter says, scratching the back of his neck awkwardly before bringing his hand down as he says, “May says it’ll be a good idea when I get to college. Test out or something.”</p><p>“Or something,” MJ says with a smile, Peter’s stomach turning to butterflies as MJ wrings her hands together.</p><p>“MJ, I--”</p><p>“I’m good at keeping secrets, you know,” MJ says, Peter pausing as he stares at her. </p><p>“Yeah, I know,” he says, thinking back to when she essentially revealed she knew his secret back in sophomore year, the spark that started their friendship and transformed him and Ned’s duo into a trio. </p><p>“But I’m okay,” MJ says carefully, “if you didn’t want certain things to stay secret anymore.”</p><p>Peter considers himself to be smart, most days-- even if Tony seemed to argue against it anytime he had to get stitched up for whatever injuries he collected while he was on patrol-- but it takes him a beat too long to understand what she’s saying as she stares at him, making the decision to lean forward and kiss him right there in the Midtown cafeteria, feet away from the trash can.</p><p>Peter’s surprised, blinking a few times as she stares at him. </p><p>“You kissed me?”</p><p>“He learns,” she says quietly, Peter huffing out a laugh before he stares at her.</p><p>“I really like you,” he says, the warning bell ringing out as a reminder that they’re still in the middle of the school day and the world hasn’t stopped to reckon with the monumental experience Peter is having as MJ smiles. </p><p>“I really like you too.”</p><p>“<em>FINALLY</em>,” three voices ring out, both Peter and MJ looking over to their friends, Gwen with her warm wrapped around Ned’s neck and Harry raising his hands in appreciation.</p><p>“Hell yeah, pay <em> up</em>,” Harry says as he brings his hand down, Gwen rolling her eyes as MJ laughs. </p><p>As their friends joke, Peter sneaks a glance back to MJ just to see her smiling at him-- a warmth blossoming in his chest and the butterflies in his stomach settling as he smiles right back at her. </p><p> </p><hr/><p> </p><p>“Incoming call from Tony Stark.”</p><p>“Put him through, Karen,” Peter says as glides through the air, flinging out another web. “Hey Tony.”</p><p>“Hey kid,” Tony’s voice comes through the comm. “How’s patrol?”</p><p>Peter lets out a long sigh. “Quiet.” </p><p>“You sound disappointed,” Tony says disapprovingly.</p><p>“Well yeah,” Peter admits. “Not that I <em> want </em>Queens to have a lot of crime, but it’d be nice for things not to be so boring too, you know? Ever since I stopped that robbery at the Gas &amp; Go last week it’s been crickets.”</p><p>This time it’s Tony’s turn to sigh. “Well, now you’ve jinxed it. Time to get my suit on. Might as well be prepared for when the alert comes through that you’ve, hell, I don’t know. Passed out mid-swing and landed in a dumpster, or something.”</p><p>Peter rolls his eyes, before swinging down into traffic just for the fun of it. “I’m gonna be fine, Tony. Like I said, it’s a boring patrol. Anyway, why’d you call? What’s up?”</p><p>“Oh not much,” Tony replies with a deceptively mild tone, Peter’s eyes narrowing in suspicion. “Just thought I’d see if the rumor that Michelle is finally your girlfriend is true.”</p><p>“What the—how do you know about that?” Peter asks, incredulous. “I haven’t even told <em> May </em>yet.”</p><p>“Ah yes, but see, you gave Karen the play-by-play earlier tonight,” Tony explains. “You should know by now that her and Fri are nothing but a bunch of gossipy old biddies.”</p><p>“Seriously, K?” Peter mutters, rounding the corner of a building, heading home. Might as well get an early start on his homework since nothing’s happening.</p><p>“Sorry, Peter,” Karen cheerfully pipes up. “I was just so excited for you, that I had to share.” Before he can reply she continues, “I am inclined at this moment to remind you that May wanted you to tell Mister Stark about your Acadec tournament this weekend.”</p><p>“Nice deflection, Kare,” Tony says appreciatively. “Acadec, Pete? What time?”</p><p>“You and I are gonna have a talk later about personal boundaries,” Peter sternly tells Karen. To Tony he says, “Yeah, it’s on Saturday, Cardozo High. Starts at ten.”</p><p>“I’ll be there,” Tony says. “We can go out for pizza afterward, the whole team. My treat.”</p><p>Peter rolls his eyes again. “You’re only offering that because you know Johnny won’t be there.”</p><p>“Your point?” Tony deadpans. Grumbling, he adds, “How you can stand to hang out with that flying fire hazard, I have no idea.”</p><p>“He’s a really good guy, underneath all the bravado,” Peter says. “Besides, it’s not his fault his brother-in-law is smarter than you.”</p><p>“Watch it, underoos,” Tony says, Peter laughing. “And Reed Richards is <em> not </em> smarter than me. Only one person has that honor, and his name is Colonel James Rupert Rhodes.”</p><p>“What about Pepper?” </p><p>“Ah, see, Pep has more <em>common sense</em> than me, which is slightly different.”</p><p>Peter hums an agreement. </p><p>“So what’s on the docket for the rest of the night? You staying out til curfew?”</p><p>“Nah,” Peter replies. “On my way back to the apartment now. Patrol is a wash, plus I have a huge econ project due Friday, and–”</p><p>Something suddenly tugs at Peter’s navel. He whips his head down but doesn’t see anything, only to look around for a threat but finding none. </p><p>“Kid? You still there?”</p><p>“Uh, yeah, yeah I’m here,” he says, still looking around even as he keeps swinging. “Just thought I felt a–”</p><p>Another tug, more insistent, this time accompanied with a weird tingling sensation. Peter glances down again, eyes going wide and breath going short when he sees a swirling white glimmer around his belly, spreading out toward his chest and legs as though trying to wrap around him. </p><p>“Pete? Peter?”</p><p>“Tony, something’s happening, I–”</p><p>The glimmer reaches his neck and crawls up over his face, Peter crying out as a blinding light assaults his vision.</p><p>“Peter, what’s happening? Pete? Listen, I’m on my way, I’m–”</p><p>Whatever else Tony was about to say, Peter never hears it, as just then the glimmer covers his ears, completely enveloping him. Out of instinct he lets go of the webs to clutch at his head, only for the thought he’s now falling toward the unforgiving pavement causing his whole body to jerk with panic.</p><p>But if he was actually falling, he never hits the ground, as just then there’s one more final tug, this time on his entire body and with a loud popping noise. The effect of it all is more intense than anything Peter’s ever felt before, and way too much for his heightened senses to handle.</p><p>Just when he feels he can’t take any more, the bright light suddenly goes black, and Peter is lost to the dark.</p>
