        <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Work Text:</h3>
          <div class="userstuff"><p>A couple of days after Shang Qinghua saves Mobei Yi from Linguang Jun’s terrible, <i>horrible</i> assassination attempt-</p><p>(Look, Shang Qinghua knows he <i>created</i> this goddamn character, and it may have been a good idea at that time, for like creative reasons, but now that Shang Qinghua has to actually live through this life, he is allowed to say this: Linguang Jun is truly The Fucking Worst™!!! </p><p>Who even tries to target <i>children</i>? Mobei Yi is still so young! And adorable! He has cute cheeks that Shang Qinghua thinks about pinching all the time, but never actually acts on his thoughts because he values his fingers! </p><p>Sure, Mobei Yi will one day become the next Mobei Jun, and sure, being the only heir from this current Mobei Jun’s first wife makes him the most logical heir to the throne, but Mobei Yi has other half-siblings! And! Shang Qinghua honestly can’t stress this enough: Mobei Yi is still <i>a child</i>!)</p><p>-Shang Qinghua gains a shadow. </p><p>It takes him the better half of the day before he realises that the feeling that someone is tailing him that he hasn’t been able to shake off all day is because, well, someone has been tailing him all day. </p><p>Credit where it’s due, his little prince moves around stealthily enough that Shang Qinghua has to pay proper attention before he figures out that it is Mobei Yi following him around. </p><p>At first, Shang Qinghua thinks that Mobei Yi is trying to silence Shang Qinghua for seeing him at his most vulnerable. It’s a very uncharitable thought that Shang Qinghua is very sorry for, but he can’t help it! This cutthroat demon world has turned him Like This against his will! But it becomes slowly more apparent through the day that Mobei Yi holds no ill will towards him. </p><p>Quite the opposite, actually.</p><p>Objects in Shang Qinghua’s path that may obstruct his path as he carries the stack of scrolls that Linguang Jun has ordered him to collate all disappear in a poof of black smoke before Shang Qinghua comes across them. The gesture makes him smile to himself, and he busies himself with his tasks, intent on letting Mobei Yi work on getting his gratitude out of his system quietly. </p><p>What a good kid! No wonder he’s Shang Qinghua’s favourite! Shang Qinghua did such a good job writing him! </p><p>He lets Mobei Yi carry on for the next two days, but when it becomes apparent that his prince doesn’t intend to stop following him around like a very helpful shadow, Shang Qinghua knows that he must speak to Mobei Yi to let him know that he’s done enough. If it keeps up, it’s bound to attract attention, no matter how sneaky his prince is being, and it really won’t do for Linguang Jun to catch wind of this. </p><p>For one, it would’ve been a win for Linguang Jun the other day, if Mobei Yi had come to harm. Shang Qinghua saved Mobei Yi, which puts Shang Qinghua’s position very clearly against Linguang Jun. If Linguang Jun finds out, he will probably order for Shang Qinghua to be skewered and barbecued for dinner. Shang Qinghua is not fond of this thought! Not at all! </p><p>Secondly, if Linguang Jun ever finds out that Shang Qinghua has earned Mobei Yi’s trust, Shang Qinghua’s next mission will probably be to gut his prince himself. Also not a good thought! </p><p>So! It’s much better that he speaks to his prince first, and to thank him for all that he’s done for him the last couple of days, and to declare that they’re now even.</p><p>“We’re not,” Mobei Yi says, petulantly determined. “You saved my life. I’ve… I’ve just been removing boxes so you don’t trip. We’re not even.” </p><p>His prince is too cute!</p><p>To be honest, Shang Qinghua hadn’t really given any thought to the concept of a life debt when he intervened to save Mobei Yi from the cultivators the other day, but of course, <i>of course</i> his stoic little prince would interpret it as such. Ah, this would be a little trickier to deal with, but Shang Qinghua will manage. </p><p>Shang Qinghua crouches down. “My prince,” he starts gently, “you don’t owe your lowly servant anything. It is my duty to protect you-”</p><p>Mobei Yi scowls. “Not my servant,” he says, tugging at Shang Qinghua’s hair angrily. </p><p>Shang Qinghua blinks, surprised. “My prince?” </p><p>“You’re not my servant,” Mobei Yi says again, which is, well, not untrue, per se, since Shang Qinghua primarily serves Linguang Jun. “I am not your master. I don’t want to be your master.” </p><p>The last bit is said quietly. A little despondently. </p><p>Perhaps… Perhaps his prince is a little lonely, and in need of a friend? Well, that would make sense. Shang Qinghua is probably the only person in the Northern Palace right now that Mobei Yi thinks he can trust, and hence the only real candidate that Mobei Yi can think of, in his pursuit of friendship. </p><p>Shang Qinghua’s heart clenches tightly at the thought, and he says a mental apology to Mobei Yi again for giving him this kind of tragic childhood backstory. It had seemed like a good idea at that time! But all Shang Qinghua wants to do now, looking at Mobei Jun’s sad little face, is to bundle the child up in many soft blankets, and to protect him from the evil that is his entire extended family! </p><p>“Are you hungry, my prince?” Shang Qinghua asks. He should be, given that he's spent the tire day running after Shang Qinghua, and using portals to get rid of items in Shang Qinghua's way.</p><p>He probably shouldn’t be seen around Mobei Yi, but it’s late enough that the kitchen staff would have retired for the night, and he rationalises to himself that Mobei Yi is going to be the next Mobei Jun, after all. Wouldn’t it be in Shang Qinghua’s best interests to hug his thighs super early on in the story? This way, when Mobei Yi becomes super OP, Shang Qinghua might gain a seat on his council (in addition to keeping his life!) as a trusted advisor! Perhaps Mobei Yi might bestow upon him some land! Cha-ching! And! He gets to make sure now that Mobei Yi’s childhood is not <i>so</i> filled with trauma! One stone! Many birds! </p><p>Mobei Yi nods gingerly. </p><p>Shang Qinghua smiles. “I’ll make you a deal,” he says.”</p><p>“What deal?” Mobei Yi asks. </p><p>“No more following me around in the day,” Shang Qinghua tells him seriously, because damn, he’s still really cognizant of the fact that they could <i>both</i> get into trouble this way. He holds a hand up to stop Mobei Yi from speaking when it looks like his prince is going to object. “If you really want to repay me, you’ll have suppers with me instead, away from prying eyes.”</p><p>Mobei Yi contemplates the offer. “Have I caused you trouble, <i>gege</i>?” </p><p>Shang Qinghua chokes on air. “My prince! You!” <i>Gege?</i> They are so far apart in rank that if anyone hears Mobei Yi call him that, he’ll probably be flogged for impertinence! “My prince, it’s probably best you don’t call me that!”</p><p>Mobei Yi’s face falls. “Will that cause you trouble also?” </p><p>Ah, what the <i>hell!</i> Shang Qinghua was not made to tolerate such cuteness! How is he supposed to not let Mobei Yi do whatever the hell he wants to do, if Mobei Yi is looking at him, eyes all wide and sad like that? </p><p>“No!” Shang Qinghua ends up saying, like the liar he is, leading his prince towards the kitchen. He wonders if his prince will like noodles. Well, Shang Qinghua hopes he does, since it’s the only thing Shang Qinghua knows how to cook. “You can call me whatever you want, as long as you like it, and as long as it’s in private!” </p><p>Mobei Yi nods. “I promise I won’t cause trouble for you, <i>gege</i>.”</p><p><i>Aiyo.</i> Shang Qinghua is going to die from heart palpitations this way. How can a single person be so cute? </p><p>“It’s fine, my prince,” he decides. “You can cause whatever trouble you want,” he tells Mobei Yi. “This Shang-<i>gege</i> will take care of everything.” </p><p>He starts regretting all his life choices the next night, when Mobei Yi sneaks into his bed to sleep with him. </p><p>(Not enough to tell him to stop, though. Shang Qinghua honestly will challenge anyone to look his sleepy little prince in the eye, and tell him to go back to his own chambers. Shang Qinghua is only a weak, little human!)</p></div>
