        <div class="summary module" role="complementary">
          <h3 class="heading">Summary:</h3>
            <blockquote class="userstuff">
              <p>Hamilton goes far and beyond to complete his mission and help his General no matter the cost.<br/>And the cost is far bigger than he thought.  </p><p> </p><p>Sorry not good at summaries.<br/>Basically  Hamilton ignores his body and gets sick. Like real real sick. Like you should never do this!<br/>(Kinda based on real events. Read the notes for further info.)</p>
            </blockquote>
        </div>

        <div class="notes module" role="complementary">
  <h3 class="heading">Notes:</h3>





    <blockquote class="userstuff">
      <p>Okay so while reading Hamilton's biography one particular moment caught my eye and i just had to turn it into a fic.<br/>So basically, Washington send Hamilton on many missions to deliver messages and convince generals to help them by lending a few brigades cause they didn't have enough soldiers.<br/>So Alexander had to make a lot of rides across the Hudson River, riding 60 miles a day, for five days, riding like a man possessed. (i love that line!) He eventually had to stay in a town  cause he got super ill. Like doctors thought he wouldn’t make it. But He did, (of course he did) and then (the idiot) went back to work, riding from place to place. Eventually he collapsed outside of Morristown, cause he was still sick and spend the next two month recovering. (Nice work dumbass!)<br/>So i am NOT retelling that part of the biography !  I am stealing a few pieces and making my own little story about Hamilton overexerting himself while going to deliver a message for Washington. SO THAT IS WHY it's kind of based on a true story, sort of.</p>
    </blockquote>

    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p align="left">
  <strong>There weren’t enough.</strong>
</p><p align="left">There weren’t enough men. That was the gist of it. No matter what the general said, that was it.</p><p align="left">There just weren’t enough soldiers, which was incredibly frustrating because Alexander knew for a fact that congress had enough money and power send 10 more brigades to help them but of course they didn’t.</p><p align="left">Of course he and Washington and the other generals had to do that <strong>while</strong> fighting the war. They were on their own, so…</p><p align="left"><br/> “So we get more.” Alexander said.</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington chuckled, shaking his head tiredly. “It’s not that simple.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “Why not?” He asked, a little irritation slipping into his voice. “All we have to do is ask General Putman for 2 more brigades to meet us halfway.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “Because Hamilton, it would take a messenger at least 5 days to reach Fishkill and General Putman and then another 5 to return.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “So he will ride fast.” Alexander piped up but Washington just glared at him for a moment before continuing.</p><p align="left"><br/> “Also he will have to convince the man to help us, which will also take time, which we do not have!”</p><p align="left"><br/> “A firm hand is all he needs.” Alexander crossed his arms and whispered under his breath.</p><p align="left"><br/> “And we will need his answer as quickly as possible…” Washington sat in his chair, pulled his hat off and ran a hand over his head.</p><p align="left"><br/> “…so I can know if I will be sending men to attack or screaming for their return.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander watched him, his need to help growing with each new crease on his leader’s brow.</p><p align="left"><br/> “Your Excellency, I can reach Fishkill in three days and come back just as fast.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington’s head snapped up and looked at his aide in confusion.</p><p align="left"><br/> “It will not be the first time you have sent me to deliver a message.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Hamilton watched the General think for a moment before shaking his head.</p><p align="left"><br/> “No”</p><p align="left"><br/> “Sir, please hear me out.” Alexander stepped up and leaned on Washington’s desk, looking him directly in the eyes.</p><p align="left"><br/> “You and I both know that a simple messenger will not be able to convince General Putman to send two brigades to help…but I can, sir!”</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington looked away, his brow frowning even more as he thought about it.</p><p align="left"><br/> “You can’t reach it in three days, Hamilton.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander couldn’t believe his ears. “Of course I can, I know the way perfectly!” He almost shouted the words.</p><p align="left"><br/> The General glared, rising from his chair and towering over his aide.</p><p align="left"><br/> “And you think the messenger who has traveled the whole country multiple times doesn’t?!”</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander, of course being as stubborn as he is only leaned closer, now having to look up at his commander.</p><p align="left"><br/> “He does not know the importance of this mission…sir!”</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington shook his head and walked away from him, going to the map he had set up in his tent.</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander couldn’t understand why Washington was fighting him about this so much. It wasn’t like he hasn’t spent days traveling from place to place negotiating before? And right now they were in a very rare position where they could for once surprise the British and attack them from behind. This was their chance. This one attack could change a lot for them.</p><p align="left"><br/> This message had to be delivered. Putman had to be convinced. They had to get those 2 brigades. And he knew if he wanted this attack to go right he had to do it himself.</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander slammed his fist on the desk. “”Please, sir!” He turned around and Washington was looking at him seriously.</p><p align="left"><br/> “What other option do we have?”</p><p align="left"><br/> The General looked away for a moment before letting out a long and tired sigh.</p><p align="left"><br/> “Fine, Hamilton, fine. You leave in an hour. Go get ready.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander didn’t even try to hold back his smirk as he saluted his General and walked out of the tent.</p><p align="left">***</p><p align="left">Precisely one hour later Hamilton was on his horse waiting outside the general’s tent.</p><p align="left"><br/> John Laurens was standing beside him.</p><p align="left"><br/> He was fidgeting and looking up at his friend from time to time with a worried expression on his face. Hamilton straightened his coat trying to ignore him but when John looked up at him for the up tenth time he sighed.</p><p align="left"><br/> “John I will be fine, I promise you.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “This is ludicrous. Delivering messages is not your job.” Laurens said with an angry tone.</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander laughed “Yes it is.”</p><p align="left"><br/> John turned to him fully looking up at his friend with a determent expression.</p><p align="left"><br/> “No, your job is to be by the General’s side…with me.” He said the last part in a lower tone but Alex heard him, which earned John a small smile.</p><p align="left"><br/> “What is not your job is giving insane ideas and…and going through with them yourself! A messenger should be doing this!” He snapped looking away.</p><p align="left"><br/> “A messenger will not be able to handle it or do it properly; you know that, my friend.” Alexander leaned down and placed his hand on John’s shoulder giving it a little squeeze.</p><p align="left">“Come now, don’t be mad.”</p><p align="left">Laurens just huffed.</p><p align="left">“Yes mon ami, you should be happy for our petit lion!”</p><p align="left">A new voice piped up and both men turned around to find Lafayette and Washington walking towards them.</p><p align="left"><br/> With a huge grin Lafayette smacked the little immigrant’s thigh which caused him to jump. “If he succeeds we will finally be able to land a heavy blow on the king’s army!”</p><p align="left"><br/> “That’s right!”</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander gave an equally excited grin after those words and both men grasped each other’s hands holding them tight.</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington chuckled beside them shaking his head. “Do not celebrate just yet boys let us first get an answer from old Putman.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “Right sir!”</p><p align="left"><br/> Lafayette and Hamilton gave firm nods. Washington stepped up and handed his aide the letter. Alexander took and put it in the inside pocket of his coat without looking away from the General.</p><p align="left"><br/> Soldiers were starting to gather around them curiously now.</p><p align="left"><br/> The General looked his aide over and his brow frowned.</p><p align="left"><br/> “Hamilton, where is your cloak?”</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander scratched the back of his neck as he murmured sheepishly. “It’s…um…too torn up sir. It will do nothing for me if it rains.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington nodded and then turned to Lafayette. “Get me my cloak will you marquis.”</p><p align="left"><br/> The Frenchman turned on his heels and went into the tent.</p><p align="left"><br/> Hamilton’s jaw fell open when his brain was able to process the words and he immediately turned to his commander.</p><p align="left"><br/> “Your Excellency, please there’s no need for that! I…I can take Laurens’s or Lafayette’s! You should…”</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington shook his head calmly. “Don’t be ridiculous Hamilton. Their cloaks are in no better shape, I’m sure.” He looked to Laurens who gave a quick nod and bit his lip to stop his smile from showing at the sight of his friend’s reddening face.</p><p align="left"><br/> “John!” Hamilton exclaimed betrayed.</p><p align="left"><br/> “Here you are, mon General!” Lafayette handed the black cloak to Washington who stepped up next to Hamilton and tossed it over his shoulders. It was so big and heavy that Alexander hunched over when its weight first fell on his shoulders.</p><p align="left"><br/> He heard snickers all around him and his face turned even redder. He looked down at his hands swallowing hard. “S..sir p-please you need this more than I do.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “Alexander.” The seriousness in the General’s tone made Hamilton look at him. He seemed… dare Alex say it, concerned?! <em>But that was ridiculous! Why would he worry for him so? He was just his aide. Yes he was smart and quick but surely Washington could find a replacement in case something happened. And he still had Laurens with him. Why would he be worried?</em></p><p align="left"><br/> The General took a step towards him and pulled the cloak forward so it could cover him fully. “I am not very keen on this idea of yours…” he said but then snapped his mouth shut for a moment as if to stop himself from saying something more.</p><p align="left"><br/> Alex gazed at him in confusion. Washington looked at him, sighed and shook his head.</p><p align="left"><br/> The next words he said so quietly only he, Laurens and Lafayette could hear. “…just take it Hamilton, it will ease my mind.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Alexander gave a wordless little nod before clearing his throat and straitening up. “Thank you sir. I promise I will not disappoint you.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “Good.”</p><p align="left"><br/> “Good luck mon ami.” Lafayette bowed his head a little with his hand on his chest and a small smile on his face.</p><p align="left"><br/> Hamilton returned the smile. “Thanks.” Then he turned to John and without missing a beat said. “I’ll be careful.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Laurens stood frozen for a moment, surprised that his words had been stolen from his mouth, before chuckling. “You better.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Hamilton snapped the reins on his horse and gave one last salute. “I’ll be back before you know I’m gone.”</p><p align="left"><br/> Washington, Laurens and Lafayette watched as he disappeared into the distance.</p><p align="left"> </p>
