            <blockquote class="userstuff">
              <p>Tommy, newly deserted Hunter doesn't quite know how it all lead up to this; Exhausted and dragging along a stumbling and completely out of it, Tubbo towards what he hopes will give them some respite. </p><p>"Thirsty," He hears Tubbo mumble against his shoulder. It need not be said, Tommy knows.</p>

  <h3 class="heading">Notes:</h3>





    <blockquote class="userstuff">
      <p>I don't really like RPF, although I'm aware that Dream SMP is technically toeing the line, so I'm very surprised that I somehow landed here. I'm a big fan of *any* platonic series (book or movie) that stay platonic without involving romance so I got hooked far too quickly in my opinion. </p><p>This is very loosely inspired by "Ruby in the Moonlight" by MatleenaMaddie and SilverWing15. And by loosely inspired by, I mean only the SBI being vampires part. So I borrowed a part of their concept really. In fact, I'm blaming a large part of the creation of this on Silverwing15's series "This House Is A Fucking Nightmare" which is what sent me tumbling down the hill of morally ambiguous SBI from the very beginning. That being said, I've chosen to take my own spin on this so don't read their works and expect it to be too similar but definitely check it out.</p><p>I'll start off laying some ground rules for this fic for mine and any readers' sake. This work will not contain any romance at all. This is not meant to represent the CCs, which is why Tubbo is blonde like his Minecraft skin. It's difficult to draw the line between the characters and the content creators but I will try my best. </p><p>That being said, every character here is questionable at best. We get some pretty screwy views on murder, kidnapping, and general mayhem that I do not actually believe in. This is just for fun but will be deleted if the CCs express discomfort towards fanfictions. There will be some blood and violence and mild gore but that is mostly done to standby Original Characters. Nothing too bad will happen description-wise to the SMP characters. The M-rating might be a bit strong but I believe in better safe than sorry! :) </p><p>ALSO! Do NOT shove this into the content creators' faces, avoid sharing this in any way that would result in them getting this, please. Based on what I've heard and researched, most are fine with there being fan works out there BUT do not want to see it which is completely fine and respectable. So please, respect the content creators, thank you!</p>
    </blockquote>

    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>
  <span>"I'm thirsty," Tubbo mumbles into Tommy's collarbone as they stumble along the streets, Tommy snarling at anyone who dares to eye them just a little too long. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I know Tubs. But we can't stop here, just a little further." Tommy stresses as he pulls the slightly shorter teen along.  </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Thirsty," Tubbo repeats as he wets his lips, eyes slightly glazed, fingers digging into Tommy's right upper arm. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy swears. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>It's early, few are out and about at this time but soon the streets will be full of people, rushing around each other. They might be wandering down one of the less busy streets, but they can't afford to be out in the open too long. They can't afford to be out during direct daylight. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Soon there," Tommy chants mindlessly, as he finally sees the rusty sign lightly swinging in the wind. "Just a little further, don't give up on me bitch-boy." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo continues to mumble something indistinguishable, face practically mushed against Tommy's shoulder, long straight strands of blond hair hanging like a curtain, hiding the pale face underneath.</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>They practically fall through the door into the dimly lit, funny-smelling reception. A sour man eyes them suspiciously, taking in Tommy's somewhat torn, muddy clothes and Tubbo's almost pristine-looking clothes. There are twigs in Tommy's hair and Tubbo, although still practically plastered against Tommy, is deathly pale. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy glares. Daring the man to say anything. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>It's silent for a moment, the man clearly weighing his options as he warily drags his cloudy eyes from one teen to the other. A hand short the middle finger taps against the dark wooden desk that is only adorned with a worn-looking leather book and a rusty, dusty bell that gleams dimly in the light. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Ya got the cash?" He asks after a moment, almost sneering. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"You got a room?" Tommy challenges, scraping at the last of his energy reserves. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"200 pounds a'night." </span>
</p>
<p>
  <span> </span>
</p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy opens his mouth in outrage, but a low, sad noise from Tubbo makes him reconsider. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Fine," he snaps as he slams down a roll of cash. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>The man eyes the rolled-up money critically, removing the rubber-band carefully to painstakingly count the amount. They stand in silence save for Tubbo's small whines that do nothing for Tommy's stress levels. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Three nights," the man eventually reluctantly allows as he turns around. He reaches out to remove one of the keys that dangle on the poorly made crooks, putting it on the table between them. Tommy snatches the key off the table, closing his fist around it as his thumbnail nervously traces the almost worn-out number 'four' messily painted on a small wooden badge. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>He begins to drag Tubbo closer to the staircase and swears. It's narrow, steep, and looks just about ready to collapse at any given moment. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"That thing you're carrying better not disturb any of the other guests," the man speaks up just as Tommy manages to hoist them onto the first step. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>He stiffens as he turns to look at the man who continues to glare at them with barely hidden disdain. Tommy grits his teeth nods once and continues to half carry, half drag Tubbo's dead-weight up the stairs. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>When they finally make it into the room that more resembles a moldy cupboard than anything else, the first thing Tommy does is deposit Tubbo on the thinnest bed he has probably ever seen. It also happens to be the </span>
  <em>
    <span>only</span>
  </em>
  <span> bed and Tommy grapples with the urge to go back down and swear up a storm. Like </span>
  <em>
    <span>hell</span>
  </em>
  <span>, this shit-hole was worth 200 pounds a night. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>But he knows he can't. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Toms," Tubbo mumbles. He hasn't moved from the uncomfortable position he had flopped into as Tommy put him down. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Yeah, I know big man," Tommy says quietly as he carefully locks the door. Not that locking it matters, one good kick would bring it down like one of Sam Nook's house of cards. But it eases a part of Tommy's brain that has saved him many times throughout the years, and thus he complies.  </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I'm thirsty, so thirsty, I think I'm dying," Tubbo babbles as he swallows drily. Nuzzling into the thin blanket that had been thrown over the bed at some point. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I know, I know," Tommy repeats uselessly as he walks over to the window and closes the curtains as well as he can. "I'll fix it, just, just, wait a moment big man." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo doesn't answer. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy exhales shakily as he drags his hand through his curls, wincing as twigs dig deeper into his scalp. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"You want water Tubs?" He asks as he digs through his backpack, ice pooling in his gut. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo is silent. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy swallows uneasily. He grabs a water bottle and two energy bars before standing up and nervously approaching the bed. He doesn't dare to throw himself into it, he very much doubts the swaying thing could take it. Instead, he carefully eases himself until he's sitting closest to the wall, back leaning against the headboard and Tubbo curled up against his leg. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"You're thirsty?" He asks, even if he </span>
  <em>
    <span>knows</span>
  </em>
  <span>. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo stirred, moving until one eye peaks up towards him.</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy knows. He knows that they would never be the same ocean blue they were before. He knows but it still hurts as one red eye peers up at him from underneath blonde bangs. It hurts to see those red eyes in the face of someone he doesn't know if he can live without. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>He hurts, but he knows that for all Tommy hurt, Tubbo probably hurts twice as much. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Come on bitch-boy. You said you were thirsty," he mumbles as he uncovers his right wrist, rolling up his long-sleeved black shirt. He's nervous, but he doesn't mind, not really, not if it means he can keep Tubbo by his side. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo looks torn. Torn between the hellish thirst Tommy has heard so much about and the heartbreak of having to feed of his friend to survive. They both know that the decision is beyond them both. But it still hurts. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I'm sorry." Tubbo rasps as he begins to sit up, swaying somewhat as he gently holds onto Tommy's wrist. "I'm so sorry."</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Don't be a pussy," Tommy says with a shaky smile. Tubbo tries to return it, trembling and with barely contained tears building up in his eyes. He opens his mouth, showing off inhumanly sharp fangs, <em>ready to-</em></span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy angrily nibbles his way through his second energy bar. He's somewhat lightheaded, feels like shit, and might throw up if anyone shakes him too hard. They had opened the window to air out the smell of metal that still lingered somewhat. Despite being a bit away from the Mainstreet, the easily identifiable sound from a bustling crowd could be heard through the window. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo was silent. He merely lay still, curled up against Tommy as he emptily gazed forward. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy forcibly swallowed the last bit of the energy bar before crumpling the paper into a ball and chucking it across the room. His stomach stirred uneasily at the sudden movement and he had to swallow back the bile. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>They sat in silence for a bit doing nothing but breathing, listening to the distant sound of a dog and squeals from excited children. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"America," Tommy suddenly says, fingers tapping against his leg. He's exhausted but also filled to the brim with nervous energy. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Too much of a distance," Tubbo mumbles tiredly. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"We can go by boat."</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"How?" Tubbo looks up at him, eyes dimming slightly as Tommy flinches at the color. He turns away, hiding his face in the blanket. "We can't go by boat, we can't go by plane, and we can't walk across the ocean. So How?" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Fine," Tommy snaps, "Dubai." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo snorts, turning slightly so he can look at Tommy again. Tommy meets his eyes, no flinching this time. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Tommy..." Tubbo mumbles, reaching out to grab onto the hand that isn't tapping up a storm on Tommy's left leg. "We might have to give up-"</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"No." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy tears his hand away from Tubbo's, lightly slapping Tubbo over the head. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I'm not giving up, you're not giving up. We can do this." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Tommy I don't want you to live a life on the run," Tubbo snaps angrily, sitting up. They can't have this discussion with one of them laying down, </span>
  <em>
    <span>with him</span>
  </em>
  <span> laying down. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy doesn't quite meet his eyes, glaring into the distance. "A bit late for that Tubs." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo looks at him closely. "What? What does that mean?"</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy still isn't looking at him. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Tommy."</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I deserted."</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo stills. What? Tommy? Deserted? No way, impossible, the sun rises in the east, Tubbo likes his bees and Tommy is a Hunter. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"No way... Surely not..." He stutters. Tubbo is tired. He's so tired, he can't. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"They wouldn't let me look for you," Tommy whispers, he's finally looking at Tubbo. Tubbo doesn't think he's ever seen him so brittle, so frail. "I can't, not without you. The Org is nothing but a snakepit, I can't live like that without you." He whispers, baring a weakness he'd never admit to just a couple of months ago. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo swallows, wetting his lips nervously as he leans back until he's propped against the headboard, besides Tommy. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Please don't go," Tommy whispers into the quiet air around them. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo smiles wetly, he can feel tears slowly trailing down his cheeks as his nose gets stuffy. In a couple of months, weeks maybe, he wouldn't even be able to cry. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"It's not really up to me, big man," he whispers as he rests his head against Tommy's shoulder. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>They fall asleep like that. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p><hr/>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Waking up is a horrible experience. His mouth is dry, his head pounding, and every bruise is flaring as Tommy slightly stretches. Every inch of his poor body is stiff and unyielding, sleeping while sitting up had done it no favors. He blinks blearily, groaning as he notes that the weak morning light was gone, instead, he can glimpse heavy dark clouds through the thin curtains. They must've slept to midday at the very least.   </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Tubbo?" He rasps as he wets his lips, reaching over to the water bottle that was stuck between his left leg and the wall. He grimaces as he unscrews the cap, downing half the bottle in one go.</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo mumbles something indistinguishable. He lifts his head slightly, peering at Tommy through half-lidded eyes before letting his head fall back on Tommy's shoulder. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Something twists in Tommy's lower stomach. He has never seen Tubbo this tired before, the slightly shorter teen was seemingly able to run on nothing but tea and sugar. He would rather die than say it out loud but he's worried. He's out of his depth and Tubbo probably knows less than he does. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Did the asshole tell you anything?" He asks. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>He can feel Tubbo stiffen slightly, immediately becoming alert at the barest mention of his Sire. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"No," he admitted bitterly. "I've been pretty out of it for the last couple of days, even if he did say something I can't remember." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Fucking hell," Tommy swears as he lets his head fall back against the wooden wall behind him. "I don't know much," he warns the other warily, "other than some very basic manners and social cues. Blood-suckers aren't known for being the sharing kind. Most information we have is more observations than fact." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I'm surprised you know anything other than killing them," Tubbo confesses even as he refuses to make eye-contact. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Fuck if I know why, the Organisation doesn't seem to know whether it wants to work with or against them. We're taught how to decapitate and how to properly burn a body, but sometimes they sprinkle in some lessons on manners, like how to properly greet them or how to tell older bastards apart from younger bastards." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo warily holds onto Tommy's hand. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy quietens.  </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Have you..." He trails off, swallowing nervously, "Have you felt... Thirsty?" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"No," Tubbo says with an unsure glance. "I... Other than earlier. It stopped when you..." He grimaces, his lower lip wobbling as he shakily inhales. "It felt weird, it didn't taste the same as what he gave me." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy frowns. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"You sure? Did it ever taste differently before?"</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo shakes his head silently. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"What, you think- you think they were giving you some prescription shit or?" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"..." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Fuck Tubbo! What does that mean?!" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I don't know!" Tubbo snaps with a snarl as he brings his legs closer to his chest. "I don't know, I don't- even know what I'm doing here, I don't know why you would- you... Argh!" He hides his face in his knees even as he keeps holding onto Tommy's hand. "Why would you do such a stupid thing?" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy feels the familiar embers of fury light up in his chest. This he knows how to deal with, this is familiar. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Fuck you bitch-boy! You're my friend, even if you're an asshole! I wasn't gonna let you rot amongst the blood-suckers!" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Don't you get it? I'm one of them now, Tommy, you won't just be able to keep dragging me around!" Tubbo almost shouts. He's always been slow to anger but he's never lacked the ferocity. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"You want to go back?!" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I don't </span>
  <em>
    <span>want</span>
  </em>
  <span> to! But what other options do I have?" Tubbo shrieks with heartbreak. They pause as someone angrily pounds on the thin wall beside them. They look at each other. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I'm not letting you go back big man," Tommy says quietly. Tiredly. "I can't. You won't have to hurt anyone, I promise." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"But I will. Don't you see? I'll hurt you, you buffoon." Tubbo says with teary eyes. "Don't try and deny it, you can't keep up. We don't know how much I need, how long I can go between... </span>
  <em>
    <span>Feedings</span>
  </em>
  <span>. We don't know anything, we're so out of our depth that we might as well cut our losses now before I do something I'll regret forever." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"You don't think they're going to force you to do something you'll regret sooner or later? I can keep up, I promise! If I survived that shit-hole I'll survive being your walking blood bank too!" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"But I don't </span>
  <em>
    <span>want</span>
  </em>
  <span> you to survive, I want you to </span>
  <em>
    <span>live</span>
  </em>
  <span>! You're free of the Organisation now, you can go wherever you want!" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"And I don't </span>
  <em>
    <span>want</span>
  </em>
  <span> to abandon you! I'll forever have to look over my shoulder because Prime knows those fuck-heads don't know when to quit anyway! Might as well do it together with my friend! You bitch!" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo opens his mouth to argue but another series of much angrier slams into the wall beside the bed stops him. They eye the wall warily, both tense as springs. No series of slams continue, no one tries to force open the door to yell at them.</span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>They cautiously relax. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"I know some... people," Tommy says after some silence. He doesn't meet Tubbo's eyes, merely tightens his grip around the other's hand. "I'll ask them, see what they're willing to share." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo looks at him with doubt. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"Tommy... You're not talking about..." </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"What other options do I have?" He snarks back, mimicking Tubbo. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"That's so incredibly foolish. You're serving them your head on a silver platter! Don't you remember what happened the last time?" </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tommy remains silent for a second too long. He begins to distractedly fiddle with his fingers and won't meet Tubbo's searching gaze. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>Tubbo knows this behavior. Knows what it means when Tommy acts like this. He watches with dread as Tommy shakily inhales before turning towards him. A slightly guilty but also stubborn look on his face as his light blue eyes clash with Tubbo's now red eyes. </span>
</p>
<p> </p>
<p>
  <span>"That wasn't the last time I saw them, Tubs." </span>
</p>
