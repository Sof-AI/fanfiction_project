    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>The first thing Tommy-</p><p>
  <em> <strike>That’s not my name. </strike> </em>
</p><p>He jerks, losing his footing, choking as his hand slams out, nails digging into bark, obsidian beneath his palm-</p><p>Dream-</p><p><em> <strike>That’s not my name bitchboy-!</strike> </em> <strike></strike></p><p>He wheezes, the world blurring around him, obsidian flaring and disappearing, air, cold, seeping down his lungs and expanding his ribs as he blinks, his left palm pressing against his -</p><p><em> <strike>What the fuck-</strike> </em> <strike></strike><em> </em></p><p>He shakes himself aggressively as he staggers forward, blindly seeking his way to the flash of blue, knees hitting the ground and body dropping forward and fingers shoving blindly at the mask over his face and-</p><p>He stares down at himself. At a blue and green eye sat side by side, freckles spilling down one side, a scar looping beneath the other, hair a mess of dirty blond coils, young- <em>too young, what the hell shut up you dickhead- </em>mask a bare surface of inky blackness.</p><p>He chokes as trembling fingers finds water with a ripple that makes him jerk it back, mouth open, panting, a wild sort of panic because-</p><p><em>What the hell did you do!?</em> Tommy howls.</p><p><em>What the fuck, what the fuck-</em> Dream gasps in panic. <em>What did you do Tommy- </em></p><p>His teeth clicks together and then he’s shoving his face into the water, letting coldness envelope the drowning choking of voices that doesn’t belong, fingers curling to feel dirt and grass crumble and bend beneath their grip and-</p><p>He opens his mouth, screaming, his brain a scrambling mash of mush, too much bouncing, split down the middle, jerking him in different directions and-</p><p>It quits abruptly, like a hand slammed over a mouth, the silence making him jerk back to fall down on his rump, eyes wide on the sky as he gasps for one choked breath after the other.</p><p><em>We nearly died.</em> A pause. <em>Again</em>.<em> </em></p><p><em> What the fuck is this. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em>It’s us but like – shoved together. </em>He blinks, dirty fingers finding and tangling in messy strands of hair with a yank. ...<em>I gotta say we could have looked worse. I mean. My genes are clearly to thank for this-</em><em> </em></p><p><em> Do you ever shut-up? </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> How are you not more freaked out about this is the better question because I’m- </em> <em> </em></p><p>He pushes himself up on gangly legs, nearly tips right back over, and there’s a strange sort of sudden anticipation inside of him but curled all wrong, distant and yet so close as a hand instinctively pats trembling first at his neck and then down the side of his naked hip-</p><p>
  <em>Why the fuck are we nude. </em>
</p><p>He grimaces and sets his attention on the closest tree, one foot following after the other, too short and too tall, too skinny and too wide. <em> </em></p><p><em>This is so weird but at least we’ve got our priorities right.</em> <em> </em></p><p>
  <em> - </em>
</p><p>Curled up in a dark cave with a single flickering torch he huddles against the pile of leaves and grass he’d scrambled together for something softer than stony ground to sleep against, hands stretched in-front of him.<em> </em></p><p>He stares at the axe in his left.</p><p>He stares at the sword in his right.</p><p><em> I mean, let’s be serious for a moment, there’s one clear choice here. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> The axe is a sophisticated weapon, Tommy. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Yeah, for a tree maybe you dickhead. </em> <em> </em></p><p>He curls his fingers tight around the handles and looks aside and up at the moon that stares blankly back at him.</p><p>-</p><p>
  <em> This is humiliating. Please get some fucking clothes, man. </em>
</p><p>He ducks, skulking along the sides of the building, fingers brushing white stone with a strange kind of fascination before his attention is caught by the flutter of colours in the wind and-</p><p><em> Take the green- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> I refuse! We’re already wearing that ridiculous mask of yours! Red- take the red man, it’s clearly the better colour- </em> <em> </em></p><p>His fingers curls around pink and there’s a rattling sort of strange silence in his mind as he drags the hoodie off and tugs it roughly over his head.</p><p>He breathes out, adjusting the mask into a more comfortable position over his face</p><p><em> Great. We look like a fucking Technoblade rip-off. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> He’s literally half-piglin. Wearing pink won’t- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Shut up, bitchboy. It’s the sentiment! The sentiment! </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> What does that even mean!? </em> <em> </em></p><p>He shakes his head with a grunt of annoyance as he grasps for a pair of pants and yanks them up his hips.</p><p>Stares as them slides down, something wheezing in the back of his head as he flushes and grasps for his sword to cut a piece of string.</p><p>-</p><p><em> Diamonds, diamonds, diamonds! </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> We need emeralds to trade with- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Suck it green boi! We’re going right for the riches here! Arm up, us! Go! Go! Go! Don’t listen to that- </em> <em> </em></p><p>He stops, staring down at the iron pickaxe in his hand with a sense of exasperation that curls tight inside of him.</p><p>-</p><p>
  <em>I feel like we should name him.</em>
</p><p>There’s a scuffle at the back of his mind as he rolls up, curling tight with a shiver despite the heat of the flames licking up his back.</p><p><em>I mean, he’s like – us, but like a worse version clearly because oh man, this is just weirdness. </em>There’s a flare of a tangle of emotions, complicated and fearful, resentful and livid with anger. <em>I can’t believe this is what I’ve been reduced to, stuck in the mind of this- this child.</em><em> </em></p><p><em> He’s like your age, Tommy. Are you calling yourself a child? </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> I mean, I am one so fucking duh. Child murderer. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> It was an accident! You think I’d want to be stuck here with you-!? </em> <em> </em></p><p><em>I bet you planned this! </em>The anger makes him jerk, eyes fluttering open, confused eyes seeking something but finding only sudden tense silence he doesn’t understand.</p><p><em>"Go back to sleep," </em>something hushes him in a soft crooning voice. "<em>You’re safe, there’s nothing to fear…" </em>His eyes slowly closes, burrowing his face down against the crook of his arm.</p><p><em> I can’t believe you’re manipulating us. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> It worked, didn’t it? So shut up. </em> <em> </em></p><p>There’s a moment of silence.</p><p><em> So, a name? </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> We’re not calling him Tommy or Dream. </em> <em> </em></p><p>
  <em> Well, clearly. That would be so very fucking strange. </em>
</p><p>-</p><p>He hauls himself up a tree, nimbly finding his footing and taking off from one branch to the other as the dark settles around him, scaling higher and higher until he finds a tree able to take him almost all the way to the sky.</p><p>He pushes his mask aside after dropping down, fishing for a red apple and biting down with a satisfying crunch.</p><p><em> You know, I’ve been thinking- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Hah. Hilarious. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em>I’m serious!</em> The moon is bright and he swings his legs idly, humming softly on a melody that comes as naturally to him as breathing. <em>Even if we get back to L’Manberg and everyone then-</em> A breath. <em>Can he even speak? And if he can speak, how the fuck are we supposed to play this out? We just waltz up to someone and go, yeah, hi, this strange child has us stuck in his fucking head-</em><em> </em></p><p>He fishes for his canteen, taking a generous sip of the water, kicking his right leg up to wiggle his bare and dirty toes, head tilting in contemplation.</p><p><em>We - he – is going to sound absolutely fucking insane.</em> A pause. <em>That’s if he even can talk.</em><em> </em></p><p><em>I’d rather not spend anymore time than necessary like this. We- </em>A frustrated noise. <em>We could, possibly, be making it worse the longer we stay like this, Tommy.</em><em> </em></p><p><em> Well what the hell are we supposed to do!? He looks like we had a fucking child, which is so gross, but who the hell is going to put one and one together to make two, huh? They’ll think he’s some strange relative to one of us and then there’ll be fucking nothing. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> … I hate to say it, but you have a point. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> So, detour? </em> <em> </em></p><p>A sigh.</p><p><em> Detour it is. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Whoop-the-fucking-doo. </em> <em> </em></p><p>
  <em> - </em>
</p><p><em> That’s a shit name and you know it, Dream. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> You got a better suggestion? </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Yeah, something normal for one! </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> There’s nothing normal about this, Tommy! Not a single ounce of normal- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> We’re setting him up for failure! I feel, I don’t know man, but I feel fucking responsible, you know? I’m setting my foot down. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Oh you’re setting your foot down, are you? Then give me something better, Tommy. Give me something better than- </em> <em> </em></p><p>-</p><p>Styx contemplates the strip of leather with a niggling sense of exasperation and smugness alike oozing down his back, humming idly as he worked.</p><p><em> I can’t believe you. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> It’s fitting. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Techno is going to laugh in our fucking faces the bastard. He’ll take one look at this unfortunate mythology-named-child and just laugh and we’ll get no help because he’ll be busy dying over the ridiculous name you’ve bestowed upon him. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> You’re being dramatic. </em> <em> </em></p><p>
  <em> Oh shut your fucking mouth before I make you I swear to fucking-</em>
</p><p>Styx straps the leather around his arm and there’s a strange and sudden silence that makes him look up and around him, anxiousness creeping through him but there’s no visible danger and he slowly focuses back at the task at hand.</p><p>Something thrums happily through him. It’s familiar, it’s good, it’s-</p><p><em> I hate you. </em> <em> </em></p><p>
  <em> At least he has a sense of style. </em>
</p><p>-</p><p>Styx is really getting the hang of mining, torches lining the walls as he delves deeper, feet quiet against the cold ground as he peers over the edge, balancing precariously for a moment before he twists the axe in his hand and drops down, burying it deep-</p><p>
  <em> Hell yeah, man! Fucking go! </em>
</p><p>The skull breaks, collapsing with a rattle of bones and a spilling of items that nimble fingers quickly sorts through, anything deemed important disappearing into his inventory.</p><p>He pauses, head turning-</p><p>
  <em>"RUN!"</em>
</p><p>He twists, feet slapping against the ground as he careens around the corner- dropping down the stairscase he’d made and slinging himself into the dark corridor with a hiss of scuttling feet behind him.</p><p>
  <em> There’s a fucking spawner- there’s a fucking spawner, fuckfuckfuckfuck- </em>
</p><p>
  <em>"LEFT!"</em>
</p><p>He balks, jerking back and there’s a hiss of frustration, heart pounding in his chest, a choked whine leaving him and-</p><p>
  <em>"It’s okay, it’s okay- just step back and then continue onwards, you can still make it-"</em>
</p><p>
  <em> It’ll be quicker-</em>
</p><p><em> Shut up, Dream. </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> Tommy- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> I said SHUT UP </em> <em> </em></p><p>Styx stumbles, the darkness tight and coiling in front of him, eyes finding a flicker of light and a torch in a distant and something urges him towards it and so he runs.</p><p>-</p><p><em> What was that? </em> <em> </em></p><p>There’s a mumble, Styx shivering as he curls tighter upon himself, toes close enough to the fire there’s blisters forming on his feet.</p><p><em> Tommy- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em> I’m fucking claustrophobic, OKAY? And somehow- somehow he must have gotten that too because I felt it and I- </em> <em> </em></p><p>There’s a choked noise, a mixture of feelings, fear and coiling mortification-</p><p><em>... Is it because- </em> <em> </em></p><p><em>What do you think?</em> Exhaustion makes Styx’s eyes flag but he jerks, nails digging into his calves-</p><p><em>"It’s okay," </em>something soothes almost grudgingly against his frail senses. "<em>You’re free. There’s nothing keeping you trapped."</em></p><p>Styx looks at the world, wide and green around him, trees dark oaken wood where they spread out into the wilderness and slowly pulls his feet away from the fire to a lick of relief as he lets himself sprawl out on his back.</p><p>He falls asleep to a lull of twisted triumph and regret alike, his mind otherwise silent.</p><p>-</p><p>Styx scrubs at his hands, humming idly as he worked the blood away from beneath his fingernails with a handful of sand.</p><p>He washes it off and ducks his head down beneath the surface of the water, giving his head a rough shake before straightening with a breath that fills his lungs, a shiver running down his back as cold air brushes over bare skin.</p><p>Styx inspects the strange patches of pink on his skin, like old scars, but he doesn’t-</p><p>He jerks, feet slipping and-</p><p>
  <em> Shit, that was mine wasn’t it? </em>
</p><p>He trembles, nose flaring, smelling-</p><p>
  <em> Does Withers even have a scent? I mean, other than gunpowder and like… cold. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Cold? </em>
</p><p><em>Yeah, I mean they’re- </em>A frustrated huff.<em> You know what, screw this. You can deal with this. You did the damage, you take care of the child. Make sure he doesn’t drown or whatever.</em></p><p><em>Suppose he has a point. </em>Styx hesitantly dips his head back into the water, giving his head a rough shake, carding through the strands of his hair and working a stubborn knot away from one of the longer tangles. <em>Don’t tell him I said that though. And don’t drown, that would be a stupid death. </em>A pause. <em>A stupider death, I guess. </em></p><p>Styx stares down at his reflection, at green and blue set in an off-kilter face, wet dark strands plastered against his forehead.</p><p><em>I guess you do look a lot like me like this. Not that- anyone would know. I think the only one who knows what I look like is… Technoblade. Because of that stupid duel.</em> There’s a silent pause as Styx wades out of the river and reaches for his black skin-tight shirt before shrugging into his pink hoodie. <em>I almost won but I suppose I have to give credit where credit is due. He beat me fair and square.</em></p><p>Styx reaches for his mask, halting, staring at the black surface with a contemplative tilt of his head and a niggling sense of <em>creativity.</em></p><p>
  <em> Oh, wait. Wait, are you-?</em>
</p><p>His knife digs into the black paint, red bleeding in its place as he carves a smile into the wood.</p><p>There’s a strange sense of wonder in his chest as he slots it over his face.</p><p>-</p><p>
  <em> What. The fuck. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Hey, he did it all on his own! </em>
</p><p>
  <em> I don’t believe you for once fucking second. I leave you with him for two minutes and you- you’re fucking infecting him! You’re making him more like you and I refuse, I refuse, I refuse- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Tommy- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> SHUT UP! SHUT UP!I HATE YOU. I HATE YOU I HATE YOU I HATE YOU- </em>
</p><p>Styx’s finger nails tears into wood with anguish, carving a path of clawed ruin over black paint and a stark red smile-</p><p>Something frantic wires through him and he jerks, scrambling back, a broken noise escaping his lips as he tucks his hands beneath his armpits and coils tight upon himself, trembling with a hiccupped noise.</p><p>Something quiet settles in his chest, a huff of tired regret following.</p><p>
  <em>"I’m sorry."</em>
</p><p>“Sorry,” Styx hiccups into his knees. “Sorry, sorry, sorry-“</p><p>-</p><p>
  <em> We’re gonna leave him with so many issues, man. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Are you saying we’re bad parents, Tommy? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> … Please never utter that sentence again. I think I’m gonna vomit. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Hey, look on the bright side! At least we know he can talk.</em>
</p><p>
  <em> He’s been muttering sorry for two hours, Dream. I don’t think I’m gonna count this one as a bright-anything. We broke the child. </em>
</p><p>A pause, a resigned sigh.</p><p>
  <em> Just- fucking do your thing. He responds better to you when he’s like- all that. </em>
</p><p>Bitterness, a sense of longing, something foul clawing for his attention before it’s smoothed out with a ghostly brush of a hand over his head.</p><p>
  <em>"Go to sleep, Styx."</em>
</p><p>“Sorry.”</p><p>
  <em>"You did nothing wrong."</em>
</p><p>“Sorry.”</p><p>
  <em>"Sssh, just go to sleep, everything is gonna be okay."</em>
</p><p>-</p><p>Styx makes a wide circle around the village in the distance, fingers drumming against the handle of his axe, eyes darting suspiciously.</p><p>
  <em> The hell, man, he’s going in the wrong direction! Turn back you idiot, we need to trade! We need fucking shoes! </em>
</p><p><em>Ah.</em> There’s a sense of sudden recognition, Styx’s bare feet carrying him further and further away.<em> That’s my fault I think.</em></p><p>
  <em> Your fault? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> - </em>
</p><p>
  <em> It worked. </em>
</p><p>Styx shoves his feet into his new leather boots, tying them tight after tucking the hems of his pants down.</p><p>
  <em> Dream? You can come back now. Mission accomplished and all that. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Right. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> What was that about anyway? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Nothing. </em>
</p><p>There’s a sceptical noise, Styx waving a spastic goodbye before hurrying his way out of the village, shoulders drawn tight and fingers brushing reassuringly over the mask-</p><p>-</p><p>
  <em> I give up. He’s clearly not going to say shit. </em>
</p><p><em>It was worth a try? At least we know he can talk now.</em> A contemplative silence follows Styx as he finishes the touches on his leather vambraces before strapping them tight against his forearms, flexing his fingers tryingly.</p><p>
  <em> Look, at least he’ll probably not die the moment we set off. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Satisfied with just one child murder, are we? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> He’ll be fine. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> You’re ignoring me. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Just fine. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Dream, you rotten bastard you can’t ignore me in this child’s head, I refuse- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Oh, so now you wanna talk? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> You say that if I have a choice in conversation partner. Just so you know, I’d trade you out in one fucking heartbeat. Like that. A snap of my fingers and you’d be gone- </em>
</p><p>Styx’s head swivels to the left, blinking at the trunk of a birch tree.</p><p>
  <em> You’re confusing our child. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Shut up. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> You just wanted me to talk! </em>
</p><p>
  <em> I take it back. Shut up. Begone you evil, evil green bastard- </em>
</p><p>-</p><p>
  <em> It’s been, what, three months now? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Since you killed us you mean? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> I did no such thing. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Then why else are we here? Huh? Riddle me that, bitchboy. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Maybe, just maybe, we’re here for a reason. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> You’re lot in life is really to fuck-up one child after the other, yeah, I can see that. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> It’s just like old times. Just you and me together. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> And our fucked-up child. </em>
</p><p>A quiet snort follows the surprised wheezing laugh.</p><p>-</p><p>
  <em> I take back everything I said, this child does have a sense of style. </em>
</p><p>Styx ties the strip of green fabric around his arm, admiring it with a curl of his lips before he straps the sword to his back and hooks the axe through a loop at his belt.</p><p>The leather armour is tight against the fabric of his pink hoodie, the smiling face in red torn on the mask, index finger dipping into the pouch on his hip to gather up a small fine blue powder.</p><p>He turns the inside of his wrist up, staring down at the compass painted in gold against leather, a single arrow painted firmly straight ahead until he brushes carefully over it and it twitches a step to the left.</p><p>There’s a new sense of determination burning through him as he reaches up and frames the mask, slotting it down over his face with a breath that puffs against it.</p><p>
  <em> Oh it’s fucking on! Finally! Go, our fucked-up child, go! </em>
</p><p>
  <em> We still need to- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Go! Go! Go! Go! </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Tommy- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> GO CHILD GO! WHOO! OFF WE FUCKING GO! IGNORE THE BORING GREEN MAN! THE WORLD AWAITS YOU! </em>
</p><p>
  <em> TomMY- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Shush, don’t ruin this for me Dream. GO! YES GO CHILD! ONWARDS! Like seriously dude, take a moment and be proud for our child. YES OFF WE GOOOOOO! I BELIEVE IN YOU! </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Oh come on- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> GO GO GO! WHOOOOOO! </em>
</p><p>
  <em> - </em>
</p><p>
  <em> So, where are we going? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> L’Manberg you daft idiot. </em>
</p><p><em>And then?</em> Styx scales the cliff, trading grass for snow that makes his nose twitch behind the mask as a shiver crawls down his back. <em>I mean, Bad is the obvious option, isn’t he?</em></p><p>
  <em> When there’s Phil? Ancient, old man geezer Phil? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> He’s a century old demon, Tommy. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Fuck off. We’re not going to your friends and pals. Besides- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> Tommy- </em>
</p><p>
  <em> BESIDES. Phil is soft. Have you seen him? I was gone for, like, a month and I came back and he’d practically adopted Ranboob. He’ll take one look at this fucked-up child of ours and do- well, he’ll do something. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> And Technoblade? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> … Worst case scenario? We’ll just make him dig a hole. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> A hole. </em>
</p><p>
  <em> The fuck do you’ve got against holes now? Is this another issue of yours Dream? Do we need to talk? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> You’re the one suggestion holes as a solution!?</em>
</p><p>
  <em> It’s a totally reasonable solution. </em>
</p><p>
  <em>hOW!? </em>
</p><p>
  <em> It’s a man thing, you wouldn’t understand. </em>
</p>
