    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p align="center">Yue Qi learns about a demon invasion the day his Shizun tells him.</p><p>The man is calm about it, though he’s as ever unsure about sending Yue Qi off. He does, in the end, Yue Qi is a stubborn child who pushes himself to the brink over and over, and he’s <em>talented</em> in ways no other disciple is. Though his sword strapped to his side is tied to his life force, Yue Qi takes another to defend himself, and he’s confident enough in his hand to hand combat to handle any demon that tries to fight him. He travels down with a few other disciples, prepared to handle the invasion. When they do get to the village, it’s slightly isolated from the world, but it’s known for its teas and lively nightlife, and when Yue Qi gets there, the town is <em>wrecked</em>. There’s blood smearing the streets, the stench of death in the air and if there were any sounds, it was only the sounds of footsteps from the disciples.</p><p>There was no evidence of demons. There was <em>nothing</em> but death.</p><p>Yue Qi and a few of the disciples split up, if to search for some demons, or perhaps a living soul. Yue Qi walked around a few buildings, alert, head held high as his eyes scanned the area around him. He hears it then, a soft <em>sob</em>, a whimper of <em>mama</em> and Yue Qi <em>moved</em>. Following the soft crying, he’s not sure <em>where</em> it’s coming from, or where he’s walking, but there’s a body of man and a woman, <em>mauled</em> and there’s a child, wearing stained red robes, sobbing. Yue Qi manages to swallow back a sick taste in his throat, when he notices <em>more</em> children laying there, as if they’ve been brutally slain. How did this one survive? Yue Qi notes the slashes against the boy's arm, the way he was pale, the sweat dripping from his forehead. He was <em>dying</em>.</p><p>Yue Qi moves fast, dipping his body forward, pressing his hand to the wound, calling out for the others, pushing his Qi into the younger boy, who grew silent, slumping against him. His body is feverish, Yue Qi notes, when the others come, rushing to help. The boy clings to him as they leave, even when he’s asleep.</p><p>The boy, whose name is Shen Yuan, is a silent, shy child, that Shizun allows to settle onto the peak as a Qiong Ding Disciple. He’s usually to himself, studying quietly, but he <em>clings</em> to Yue Qi. Yue Qi doesn’t know how to feel, but there’s a fondness in his chest, an inability to ignore a small boy who looks up at him with awe and interest. He usually sticks to wrapping his fingers around the edge of Yue Qi’s robes, as the elder gently instructs him on what to do and how to do it, and though Yue Qi feels the burn of guilt, of a child he once promised to return to but never did, Yue Qi had to brush it off, every time he was around Shen Yuan. They weren’t the same person.</p><p>Shen Jiu was cruel and vicious. Shen Yuan was <em>gentle</em>, and looked to Yue Qi like he was <em>his whole world</em>. That’s all Yue Qi ever wanted, was to be <em>needed</em> like that again. Like there was an empty spot in his heart and Shen Yuan had settled himself there like he fit perfectly. A whisper in the back of his mind says <em>yes</em>, <em>yes he does</em>. Yue Qi doesn’t ignore it, he embraces that strange need to continue pulling Shen Yuan <em>closer</em>. He can’t lose anyone else.</p><p>Shen Yuan has nightmares.</p><p>Some days, he doesn’t sleep. Yue Qi notices it, can see it in the way he holds himself, or the way his eyes seem dimmer than before. Yue Qi doesn’t know how to help those days, and he wonders how <em>wrong</em> it would be to tug Shen Yuan into bed with him… he does it anyway. He gently brushes Shen Yuan’s hair back one evening, asks, “does Xiao Yuan sleep well at night?” and Shen Yuan shakes his head <em>no</em>. “Would you like to sleep with Qi-ge tonight?”</p><p>Shen Yuan nods his head.</p><p>He’s always such a silent boy. Like he was afraid of talking. Yue Qi wonders what goes through Shen Yuan’s head, why he never tries speaking up, or why he simply prefers to squeeze Yue Qi’s hand whenever they’re together when he’s too stressed, or too tired, or if the lessons aren’t making sense to him. He <em>talks</em>, but it’s usually after he’s spoken to directly.</p><p>Always speaking tiredly.</p><p>The first night they rest in the same bed, some of the disciples look at him strangely. But no one says anything. Not when they know Shen Yuan cries in his sleep, when he rolls and turns, as if fighting <em>something</em>. The first night, Shen Yuan does cry. He does twist and turn, until Yue Qi rolls onto his side, presses a hand to the other’s back, rubs circles until Shen Yuan calms down, and then he <em>rests</em>. They sleep together every night after that. Their Shizun is concerned at first, unsure how to react to knowing his Head Disciple sleeps with his <em>shidi</em>.</p><p>“We don’t do anything.” Yue Qi protests, cheeks burning. “He sleeps better with someone!”</p><p>“Don’t let it get out of hand.” And it’s all Yue Qi gets, the only warning he’s told before Yue Qi continues to do it anyways. Shen Yuan rests peacefully beside him every night, and it’s enough for him. Knowing someone is relying on him again. That someone <em>wants</em> him around, no insults, no broken promises. Shen Yuan never clings to his side, and that’s fine. Yue Qi is fine laying there, staring at the curled up boy, whose hand is wrapped in Yue Qi’s bigger one.</p><p>Whenever they wake up, Shen Yuan blinks up at Yue Qi, rubs his face and yawns. It’s cute. Yue Qi notes, it’s <em>adorable</em>. He’s smitten.</p><p>He knows this.</p><p>He doesn’t mind.</p><p align="center">Some part of Yue Qi has already told himself he’s willing to go to the end of the depths of hell to keep Shen Yuan by his side.</p>
