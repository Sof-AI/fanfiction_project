      Suicidal Misunderstandings
    </h2>
    <h3 class="byline heading">
      <a rel="author" href="/users/nevertheless_turtle/pseuds/nevertheless_turtle">nevertheless_turtle</a>
    </h3>


        <div class="summary module" role="complementary">
          <h3 class="heading">Summary:</h3>
            <blockquote class="userstuff">
              <p>Obi-Wan scarcely a few years into his desert exile is (quite unexpectedly) thrown back in time to the height of the Clone Wars. This is, in fact, SO unexpected that he reasonably (if incorrectly) assumes he is experiencing a spice-induced hallucination. After enjoying spending time with his lost loved ones, he decides he really needs to wake up before he dies of dehydration. When intense meditation fails, he tries to break the illusion through more...EXTREME measures (check the tags). Panic from all parties ensues.</p>
            </blockquote>

    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>Ben sat in his lonely hovel, anxiously waiting for the spice to kick in. Should he take more? He had been rather too embarrassed to ask whether he was supposed to smoke or snort the substance...</p><p>Oh wait, there we go, he was beginning to feel...<em>odd</em>...a little...relaxed...he could see his body! <em>oh he did not look so good at the moment did he...</em>he could see his hut drifting away...</p><p>he could see...He could <em>See</em>, he Could <strong><em>S E E </em></strong></p><p><em>I - N - S - I - D - E  </em>-  <em>T - I - M - E</em> <strong> - </strong></p><p>Cody had just started to enjoy his <em>extremely</em> rare 'off-duty' time with Ghost Company when the General abruptly passed out, scarcely four drinks in. He woke almost immediately, looking around wildly. </p><p>"Sir? Sir are you alright?" Cody asked, concerned.</p><p>"I'm...not sure. This is all...this is much more <em>vivid </em>than I was expecting. Am I dead?"</p><p>General Kenobi grew worried, brow furrowing suddenly.</p><p>"You're not dead, right Cody? I—Oh dear, maybe this was a mistake..." He spun in a circle, staggering. "Is this <em>Coruscant? </em>How strange..."</p><p>Cody exchanged an alarmed look with Waxer. "I'll get him to the Speedervan—you get Hotfoot," he ordered.</p><p>Waxer nodded, slipping to the side. They were nearly at the van when Obi-Wan stopped mumbling under his breath and turned to speak clearly to Cody.</p><p>"I don't blame you for trying to kill me, you know."</p><p>Cody stumbled and Waxer made an alarmed noise. </p><p>"Sir!" Cody tried to protest, but Obi-Wan cut him off with a finger pressed to Cody's lips.</p><p>"I mean it!" he said, as they clambered together into backseat. "I'm just happy to see you all!"</p><p>"We're happy to see you too, General," Waxer replied dutifully, activating the protective forcefield for the backseat.</p><p>Hotfoot pulled into the air and began the harrowing task of navigating numerous levels directly upwards to the temple. Fortunately, the General's clearance allowed them access to priority lanes, but if the Jedi kept up like this it was going to be a <em>very</em> long journey.</p><p>“Truly Cody, I know you would never fire at me if you were you; I think Palpatine must have been controlling your minds somehow. I swear, I’ll—I’ll try and find you—I—I’m so sorry,” he said leaning into Cody’s space, starting out earnest and becoming unsettlingly intense.</p><p>“Sir?” Cody responded nervously, but Obi-Wan was distracted by the view out the speedervan’s window.</p><p>“Are you taking me to the temple?” he asked hollowly. </p><p>"Yes sir, I think you’ve had a few too many drinks, you’re—to be blunt, you’re not making any sense and it's a bit alarming. I think it would be best if you slept things off in your personal quarters for a bit,” the commander answered tensely.</p><p>Obi Wan was quiet for several minutes, growing increasingly subdued the higher they travelled. </p><p>“Will he be there? Will—will he—will <em>Anakin</em> be there?” he finally asked.</p><p>“I believe the 501st is still on leave as well. I can comm him if you wish?” he offered, slightly relieved at the prospect of passing whatever the kriff was going on with his General to someone with more specialized intel on the subject.</p><p>Obi-Wan grew quiet again. “Yes,” he whispered suddenly. “I want to see him, force help me, I still want to see him. I—I <em>mis</em>s him.” And, at that, to everyones horror, he began tearing up.</p><p>Cody fumbled to activate his comm. “Commander Cody to General Skywalker. General Skywalker, I am requesting immediate response. I repeat, Commander CC-2224 to General Skywalker, I am requesting—”</p><p>“This had better be life-or-death Cody. I’m on leave. You’re on leave. Why are you contacting me and not Obi-Wan?” General Skywalker whined over the audio channel.</p><p>“It’s about General Kenobi, sir.”</p><p>“Is he injured?” General Skywalker responded sharply, suddenly laser focused.</p><p>“He’s—he’s crying, sir.” And he was. The sound of General Skywalker’s voice had turned the trickle of tears into full blown sobs. He was clinging to Waxer; both looked on the verge of a panic attack.</p><p>“He’s <em>what.</em>”</p><p>“He’s crying, and he said he missed you. <em>Please</em> tell me you’re at the temple, sir,” Cody all but begged.</p><p>“Is this supposed be a practical joke?” Skywalker replied, annoyed. “Did Rex put you up this?”</p><p>“This is not a joke. He blacked out in the bar, started rambling about forgiving us for trying to kill him, and now he’s crying.” For additional proof he held out the comm to General Kenobi, who just continued to sob incoherently.</p><p>“Is he drugged?” Skywalker asked. There was the sound of rapid shuffling in the background, hopefully him moving to meet them at the temple gates.</p><p>“Unknown, sir. I only saw him consume alcohol, but not enough to fully account for his…altered state.” </p><p>“Are you recording him?” Skywalker’s voice was muffled by…the sound of traffic? Dank farrik, was he not at the temple?</p><p>“Am I <em>what?</em>” the commander replied indignantly. </p><p>“Kidding!” Anakin quickly shot back. “Anyway I’m 5 minutes out from the temple, you?</p><p>He conferred with Hotfoot, dutiful designated driver who, based on his white knuckles, was focused with mission-level intensity on the singular goal of navigating Coruscant traffic. </p><p>“We’ll arrive at the secondary temple gate in 10. Any orders on how to…mitigate the situation?” A quick glance at Kenobi revealed that Waxer had proceeded to patting him on the back like an overwhelmed cadet. It was unclear whether it was helping or not.</p><p>“Oh, I have no idea,” Anakin answered, overly gleeful. “I can’t even <em>remember</em> the last time he cried. And his alcohol tolerance is unfairly ridiculous so I have no idea if this is normal for him when he’s drunk!”</p><p>With that piece of unhelpful intel the vehicle lapsed mostly into silence, broken only by the sound of Kenobi’s hiccuping sobs. </p><p>Anakin called to confirm he was waiting in the top entry bay at the secondary temple gates. He paused for a moment, empty static coming over the comm. “Did he really say he missed me?” </p><p>Cody closed his eyes for a moment. He was not designed to deal with this dammit. He just wanted to go drinking with his—his fellow officer and maybe see the man he—he <em>respected</em> relax a little. He knew the Jedi were stressed by the war but if this is what a Jedi dealing with feelings looked like then—then <em>forcedammit</em> this is what a Jedi relaxing looked like!</p><p>They should be <em>honored</em> that the Generals trusted them enough to let their guard down. Clearly Obi-Wan needed his younger vod’s support beyond what he had realized. He should have known, considering they were, in a way, a squad of two for over a <em>decade</em>. He would have to do his best to support Obi-Wan now the best he could.</p><p>“Yes sir. When he realized we were bringing him back to the temple he asked if you would be there. I offered to comm you, he confirmed audibly that he wanted to see you, and that he missed you. That's—that’s when the crying began. We made contact immediately after.”</p><p>“Oh.” Skywalker left the channel open between them. Cody could distantly hear the sound of pacing on the other end.</p><p>Obi-Wan was staring at the commlink in Cody’s bracer with an unreadable expression. He swallowed a few times, and, hand shaking visibly, reached out to pull Cody’s wrist close. “Anakin? Is that Anakin? My Anakin?” Obi-wan’s voice shook along with the rest of his body.</p><p>“<em>Your</em> Anakin huh?” came the retort, in a reflexively teasing tone. “What happened the ‘dangers of possessiveness and attachment’ you’re always lecturing me on?"</p><p>Obi-wan responded, to Waxer and Cody’s mixed emotions, by shifting bodily over to Cody, clinging onto his arm for dear life and bending over to press his  forehead to the comm unit, letting out a keening sob.</p><p>“Um, Master? Was that you?” Anakin squeaked out with characteristic helpfulness.</p><p>“I don’t want to see the temple burning,” Obi-Wan rasped into Cody’s bracer. “I want to see you but I don’t want to see the bodies in the temple, not again, please Anakin I don’t want to look at them again. Anakin I just want to see you not—not—not—” and it was almost a relief when the chilling words broke down into dry sobs. Cody attempted to platonically pat him on the back with his free arm, but considering how he was clinging to Cody’s comm unit, it turned into an awkward full body hug that Obi-Wan melted into, desperately clutching at Cody and choking back small wretched noises.</p><p>“Oh kriff, did you have a vision, Master? Is that what this is about? The temple’s fine, I’ll show you when you get here, there’s no bodies here, I <em>promise</em>.”</p><p>Obi-Wan just shook, curling up halfway onto Cody’s lap.</p>
