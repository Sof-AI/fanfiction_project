      With Nothing On My Tongue
    </h2>
    <h3 class="byline heading">
      <a rel="author" href="/users/sunshine_sparrow/pseuds/sunshine_sparrow">sunshine_sparrow</a>
    </h3>


        <div class="summary module" role="complementary">
          <h3 class="heading">Summary:</h3>
            <blockquote class="userstuff">
              <p>Naruto realizes the ability of the Kage Bunshin the night after being attacked by Mizuki. At first he just wants to use them to help him train; then the true usefulness of the jutsu begins to reveal itself.</p>
<p>Featuring baby badasses, Tank Team Seven, snarky dogs, a special guest appearance by Kakashi's Guilt Complex, and many, many fix-it cliches.</p>
            </blockquote>
        </div>

        <div class="notes module" role="complementary">
  <h3 class="heading">Notes:</h3>





    <blockquote class="userstuff">
      <p>I love Naruto. Really, I do. The worldbuilding, the characters, the sheer richness and depth of the franchise thrills me on a daily basis. That being said, there are some incredibly huge plotholes in the canon, which is only to be expected when writing a 700-chapter behemoth over the course of more than a decade.</p>
<p>Usually, I can deal with plotholes. I am very good at suspending disbelief and merely enjoying the story as it comes. But sometimes they are so egregious that they really get my back up, and one of the biggest is the 'Oh Shit' plothole that is Naruto's use of kage bunshin. By 'Oh Shit', I mean that Kishimoto-sensei and his creative team went 'Oh shit!' halfway through Part II when they realized that they had to figure out a way for Naruto to train really quickly and learn a ton of stuff in a short period of time. To do this, they tried to sell us the idea that Naruto was so stupid that he <i>didn't realize up until this point that he was receiving the memories of his dispersed clones.</i></p>
<p>I understand why they did this. It was a quick, easy fix, and developed the world of Naruto a little more. But I've never bought into the idea that Naruto is stupid, or oblivious, or unobservant. Yes, he doesn't understand a lot of the stuff around him; I attribute this to him being an extremely kinesthetic learner, as well as having an absolutely shit upbringing with zero parental influence. I've studied childhood development, and there are empirical reasons to why children with supportive, educated parents do better in school than children whose parents spend less time with them and can't support them in their studies (not necessarily through any fault of their own). Naruto had zero support in his education growing up; that does not make him stupid.</p>
<p>Tl;dr I really wanted to address the massive plothole of Naruto not realizing the kage bunshin's abilities until halfway through the series. Also, I wanted to write a self-indulgent fix-it. I have like three more of these sitting in my google docs waiting for me to finish them. OTL</p>
<p>WARNING! WARNING!<br/>There will be yaoi, and yuri, and graphic sex (probably of both). However, that Slow Burn tag is there for a v. v. good reason. We will not be earning our Mature rating for anything other than violence for a loooong time.</p>
<p>Also, I speak Japanese. I use Japanese polite suffixes and will probably use Japanese words and terms in the future. If you ever have any questions as to why I'm using something in a specific way, drop me a line and I'll explain it to you. If I use anything that is not in common use in the fandom already, I'll leave translations at the bottom of the chapter.</p>
    </blockquote>

<p>Because no one had taught Naruto how to read. He‚Äôd picked up the basics in class, of course, but with no one at home to help him practice, no books of his own to practice <em> with</em>, and not even any writing utensils to practice that either, all combined with his natural energy and short attention span, writing and reading had fallen far to the wayside. <p>Naruto downed the last of his water and scowled, setting his chipped glass down carefully in the sink. <em> Well, I‚Äôll show them, </em> he decided, heading back to his bedroom to get dressed. <em> I‚Äôll learn everything so fast, it‚Äôll blow their minds! </em></p>
<p>Then he winced as his stomach, unhappy with its meager breakfast of a single glass of water, growled. <em> But maybe first I should go shopping. </em> Then he perked up. <em> Is there a way for my shadow clones to help me get more food? </em></p>
<p align="center">‚Äî</p>
<p>Naruto spent all day practicing with his clones. He‚Äôd left five in his apartment, studying hastily-borrowed books about reading from the library and practicing his writing as well. Then he‚Äôd sent a couple off to do shopping, and popped into a bakery for some breakfast of his own. The owner had sneered but taken his money, and Naruto had bounced along to the training grounds energetically, munching on his curry bread as he went.</p>
<p>When he found an empty training ground, one of the basic ones with no special terrain, he brushed the last crumbs from his hands and placed them into Tiger. ‚ÄúRight!‚Äù he said determinedly. ‚Äú<em>Kage Bunshin no Jutsu</em>!‚Äù</p>
<p>And with a multitude of <em> pop-pop-pops</em>, twenty clones poofed into existence around him.</p>
<p>Naruto blinked, momentarily taken aback by just how much <em> orange </em> was surrounding him. ‚ÄúHuh,‚Äù he said, cocking his head to the side. ‚ÄúHas my jumpsuit always been that bright?‚Äù Then he shrugged, disregarding it. ‚ÄúWell, whatever! Training time!‚Äù He turned to his clones and began barking out orders.</p>
<p>In short order, the field was filled with shouts and grunts and the sounds of flesh hitting flesh as some clones practiced the Academy taijutsu katas, some clones sparred against each other, and other clones practiced throwing kunai and shuriken into the posts at one end of the field. Naruto observed it all happily, fists propped on his hips and a grin on his face.</p>
<p>‚ÄúI‚Äôm gonna be the best ninja in the village! Believe it!‚Äù he declared, and then dove in.</p>
<p align="center">‚Äî</p>
<p>Any shinobi passing by Training Grounds 1 - 14 that day were drawn to stop by Training Ground 5, attracted by the burning chakra and the sounds of what seemed like an entire platoon of ninja practicing. Many of them blinked and watched with mild interest for a while before moving on, but some stayed longer than others, observing the flowing mass of orange with analytical eyes. One bandanna-wearing shinobi, a senbon clenched between his teeth, watched for almost an hour, dark eyes darting around the field, clearly wanting to say something. But even he too left after a while, giving up and ambling off into the maze of streets.</p>
<p>Naruto didn‚Äôt particularly care about his observers. He‚Äôd actually not even noticed them at first, focused as he was on training, until one of his clones had brought it to his attention. Then he‚Äôd shrugged and redoubled his efforts, determined to show them how serious he was about becoming a ninja.</p>
<p>It wasn‚Äôt until someone shouted his name over the noise that Naruto stopped, panting and sweating, and turned to look along with all of his clones.</p>
<p>Iruka-sensei was standing at the edge of the field, pale-faced and clearly in pain, leaning on a cane with one hand. ‚ÄúIruka-sensei!‚Äù Twenty voices bellowed, and all of the Narutos converged on their beloved teacher, hovering around him and asking questions, tripping over each other to make sure he was all right.</p>
<p>‚ÄúNaruto‚Äî‚Äù Iruka said, voice lost in the din. ‚ÄúNaruto! NARUTO!‚Äù he shouted, and the clones immediately cringed, recognizing that tone of I-am-<em>this</em>-close-to-losing-my-patience in his sensei‚Äôs voice. Silence fell, and Iruka said calmly, ‚ÄúI can‚Äôt understand you when you all talk at once. Where‚Äôs the real Naruto?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúAhahaha,‚Äù Naruto chuckled sheepishly, and dismissed his clones. He had just enough time to see Iruka‚Äôs concerned face before a sledgehammer slammed itself into his skull, and he passed out.</p>
<p align="center">‚Äî</p>
<p>‚ÄúOooh‚Ä¶‚Äù Naruto groaned, lifting a hand to his pounding skull. That had <em> hurt</em>. He cracked his eyes open carefully, squinting against the sun. It seemed like he was lying on the grass in the training field, where he had passed out.</p>
<p>‚ÄúAwake?‚Äù a voice asked, and Naruto turned his head gingerly to see Iruka sitting next to him, looking worried. ‚ÄúYou were only out for a few seconds. Any longer and I would have taken you to the hospital. Are you okay, Naruto?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúYeah,‚Äù Naruto grunted, sitting up slowly. His headache was already receding, his brain busily rearranging the load of information he‚Äôd just received. ‚ÄúSorry, Iruka-sensei. I guess I wasn‚Äôt prepared to get all of that information at once. Next time I‚Äôll let them go in groups or something.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúThat would probably be for the best,‚Äù Iruka-sensei said dryly. ‚ÄúSo, you‚Äôve been practicing?‚Äù</p>
<p>Naruto perked up instantly. ‚ÄúYeah! I figured out that when I let the Kage Bunshin go, I get all of their memories! So I can use it to train and stuff, and I don‚Äôt need anybody to practice with!‚Äù</p>
<p>Iruka chewed on his lower lip, gaze considering. ‚ÄúWell, Naruto, you certainly <em> can</em>, but it‚Äôs also a good idea to have a variety of opponents, otherwise you‚Äôll only get good at beating one style.‚Äù</p>
<p>Naruto scowled, playing with the grass and not looking at his sensei. ‚ÄúWell, nobody wants to train with me, so I‚Äôll just do it myself.‚Äù</p>
<p>Iruka let out a sigh. ‚ÄúAnd also, Naruto, it‚Äôs good to have somebody there to watch you, otherwise if you have a mistake in your form or something, you‚Äôll just keep on making that same mistake over and over again, until you‚Äôve memorized it.‚Äù</p>
<p>Naruto scowled harder, hunching his shoulders down sadly. ‚ÄúNothin‚Äô I can do about that,‚Äù he said quietly. ‚ÄúDon‚Äôt have a teacher, do I.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúYes, it‚Äôs not like you know any <em> teachers </em> who would be <em> willing to help you</em>,‚Äù Iruka said pointedly, and rapped Naruto gently on the head with his knuckles. ‚ÄúSilly.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúHey!‚Äù Naruto pouted, reaching up to rub his head. Then the penny dropped, and he stared at Iruka, mouth falling open. ‚ÄúIruka-sensei, would you‚Äîwould <em> you </em> teach me?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúWell,‚Äù Iruka said, ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt have any classes now, but I am busy with the team assignments. We‚Äôve got a week until you get your jounin sensei, and frankly, Naruto, you‚Äôre <em> way </em> behind the other students. You‚Äôll have to work really hard if you don‚Äôt want to hinder your team.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúI won‚Äôt!‚Äù Naruto shouted, bouncing in his seat. ‚ÄúI won‚Äôt hinder them, believe it!‚Äù He paused, and then asked sheepishly, ‚ÄúWhat does ‚Äòhinder‚Äô mean, sensei?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúSilly,‚Äù Iruka said again, but it was fond. ‚ÄúIt means to hold back or to make something difficult. If you hinder your team, it means you cause them problems and hold them back, rather than helping them.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúI <em> definitely </em> won‚Äôt,‚Äù Naruto vowed, eyes alight. ‚ÄúSo will you help me, sensei? Huh?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúAll right,‚Äù Iruka said, the corners of his eyes crinkling as he smiled. ‚ÄúI can‚Äôt spar with you, because I‚Äôm not healed yet, and I have to go to school in the morning‚Äî‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúThat‚Äôs right!‚Äù Naruto shouted, aghast. He couldn‚Äôt believe he <em> forgot</em>! ‚ÄúIruka-sensei, are you okay? Why are you out of the hospital‚Äîow!‚Äù He rubbed his head again sulkily.</p>
<p>‚ÄúDon‚Äôt interrupt people when they‚Äôre talking, Naruto,‚Äù Iruka told him sternly. ‚ÄúI‚Äôm fine, I was released this morning, I just have to take it easy for a couple days. As I was saying, I have to go into school in the mornings, but I should be free after lunch. Can you study by yourself in the morning, and then meet me in the afternoon?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúSure!‚Äù Naruto beamed. This was <em> awesome</em>! He was going to get Iruka-sensei all to himself!</p>
<p>‚ÄúI‚Äôm going to work you really hard, Naruto,‚Äù Iruka-sensei said ominously. ‚ÄúYou don‚Äôt know a lot of the stuff genin are supposed to know, so it‚Äôs a really good thing you know the Kage Bunshin, because you‚Äôre going to have to do a lot of catching up on theory too. Do you still have your textbooks?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúAh‚Ä¶‚Äù Naruto ducked his head. ‚ÄúSome of ‚Äòem,‚Äù he mumbled.</p>
<p>‚ÄúWhat do you mean?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúWell. Well, some of ‚Äòem kinda got destroyed, and I kinda‚Ä¶never had some?‚Äù Naruto stared determinedly at the grass, digging a little hole with one finger.</p>
<p>‚ÄúWhat do you <em> mean</em>?‚Äù Iruka asked again, and his tone of voice promised Very Bad Things. Naruto cringed. ‚ÄúSome of them got <em> destroyed</em>?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúIt‚Äôs no big deal, sensei,‚Äù Naruto said quickly, as if saying it faster meant that maybe Iruka-sensei wouldn‚Äôt understand him and he wouldn‚Äôt get in trouble. ‚ÄúSometimes people take my stuff, or they‚Äôd write on my textbooks and tear pages out but I learned real quick to hide ‚Äòem better so I still have the ones from the last couple years but just, uh, the first few years at the Academy I don‚Äôt really know what we learned‚Äî‚Äù</p>
<p>‚Äú<em> What</em>,‚Äù Iruka said calmly. Naruto carefully edged back a few feet.</p>
<p>‚Äú‚ÄîAnd sometimes I don‚Äôt really understand the words and stuff but I‚Äôm practicing, sensei, I promise, I got five clones in my apartment right now working on reading so please don‚Äôt say you won‚Äôt train me!‚Äù he begged. ‚ÄúI promise I‚Äôll work really hard! Just‚Äî‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúNaruto,‚Äù Iruka said gently, ‚ÄúI‚Äôm not mad at you. Just slow down and tell me what happened.‚Äù</p>
<p>Naruto took a deep breath. ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt have anybody at home to help me, so sometimes I don‚Äôt‚Ä¶really understand the homework. And sometimes people take my stuff and I don‚Äôt always get it back, so I couldn‚Äôt always do the homework anyway. But it‚Äôs okay, sensei, I usually figured it out‚Äî‚Äù</p>
<p>Iruka held up a hand, and Naruto fell silent. ‚ÄúI will give you new copies of the textbooks,‚Äù Iruka said quietly. ‚ÄúAnd if anyone <em> ever </em> takes your things maliciously again, Naruto, I want you to beat them up. You‚Äôre a ninja, nobody should be taking your things. Now. What is this about having clones practicing reading?‚Äù</p>
<p>Naruto flushed a dull red, and immediately looked back down at the ground. ‚ÄúI told you,‚Äù he muttered, a hot swell of shame rising in his chest, ‚ÄúI don‚Äôt have anybody at home to help me practice.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúSo you‚Äôve been struggling with reading this <em> whole time</em>?‚Äù Naruto couldn‚Äôt tell if Iruka-sensei was mad at him, or at someone else, but he was definitely mad about <em> something</em>. ‚ÄúNaruto, why didn't you tell someone?‚Äù</p>
<p>Naruto shrugged. ‚ÄúDidn't think it'd make a difference,‚Äù he said honestly.</p>
<p>Iruka‚Äôs lips thinned. ‚ÄúWell, now you have me,‚Äù he declared.</p>
<p align="center">‚Äî</p>
<p>True to his word, Iruka drove Naruto hard over the next three days, assigning massive amounts of reading for the genin‚Äôs mornings and then quizzing him on what he‚Äôd read during their training in the afternoons. He didn‚Äôt stint on the praise either, congratulating Naruto on his improvements, of which there were many.</p>
<p>In the first day, Naruto managed to finish all of the material from the first year at the Academy and improved his taijutsu and weapons-throwing remarkably. The second day, he finished all of the second-year material and progressed to practicing ninjutsu along with taijutsu. The third day, he finished all of the third-year readings, but they didn‚Äôt get through all of the material because Naruto begged Iruka to start teaching him the basics of sealing, which had finally been mentioned in his textbooks.</p>
<p>Iruka caved after a brief resistance, and allowed Naruto to check out several scrolls on the basics of sealing from the library. Naruto set a few clones to study them as he and the rest of his small army began working on stealth and trap-laying‚Ä¶</p>
<p>‚Ä¶And got completely <em> schooled </em> by Iruka-sensei, who was apparently some kind of trap-laying, stealth-master expert!</p>
<p>‚ÄúIruka-sensei, it‚Äôs not fair,‚Äù Naruto whined, flopped over the trunk of a fallen tree where he‚Äôd landed after triggering a mild explosion trap. Most of the clones who were helping him had poofed out of existence, destroyed by various other traps spread throughout the training ground, and he just wanted to lie there for a little while and assimilate all of his memories. ‚ÄúHow are you so good at this?‚Äù</p>
<p>Iruka chuckled lightly, landing silently next to Naruto‚Äôs prone form and squatting down next to him. ‚ÄúI am a chuunin, you know,‚Äù he said reasonably. ‚ÄúAnd I‚Äôve been doing this for a lot longer than you have. Besides, you‚Äôre quite good, too, Naruto. You‚Äôve evaded top jounin and even some ANBU squads before. You just need more practice, and you‚Äôll be as good or better than I am soon.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúNot if I keep wearing this,‚Äù Naruto sighed, plucking at his orange jumpsuit morosely, sending up a puff of purple powder. ‚ÄúI just make a <em> bigger </em> target of myself. I guess I should go buy some stealthier clothes, huh.‚Äù</p>
<p>Iruka blinked, surprised. That was a surprisingly mature and self-aware thing for Naruto to say. And it was completely unexpected. Was this the effect of the shadow clones already, making Naruto smarter and more aware? Iruka <em> had </em> noticed a decrease in the time it took the young blond to assimilate the information he received, just over the past couple of days. Where he used to glaze over and stare into space for a while, clearly conveying that something was going on, now Naruto just looked off into the distance for a little bit, sometimes frowning or grimacing depending on the memories he was receiving. Iruka had no doubt that with more practice, Naruto would be able to receive and organize vast amounts of information very quickly and almost unnoticeably.</p>
<p>And what, precisely, did that mean for Naruto‚Äôs brain development? Iruka thought suddenly. He was effectively experiencing exponentially more hours in the day than usual. Did that mean that all of that experience, all of the decision-making and critical thinking and mental processing, was making him mature faster?</p>
<p>It was definitely something to keep his eye on. If it was true, Naruto could potentially learn in a year what it took other shinobi ten years to learn, but he could mature too fast, learn too much, and not learn the other important lessons that went along with it, such as teamwork and relying on others and how to make the hard decisions that shinobi were inevitably forced to make.</p>
<p>‚ÄúIruka-sensei?‚Äù Naruto asked, and Iruka blinked, looking down at where Naruto had craned his neck around to look up at his teacher, brow furrowed. ‚ÄúYou okay? You went really quiet all of a sudden.‚Äù</p>
<p>Iruka shook himself out of his thoughts and smiled, ruffling Naruto‚Äôs hair and ignoring the puff of green powder and the squawk this elicited. ‚ÄúI‚Äôm fine, Naruto, just thinking about some stuff. We‚Äôre about done for today; do you want some help shopping? I can give you advice on good shinobi wear.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúYeah!‚Äù Naruto cheered, getting eagerly to his feet and grinning brightly. ‚ÄúAnd can we get ramen afterward, Iruka-sensei? Pleeease?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúI suppose,‚Äù Iruka chuckled fondly. ‚ÄúWhy don‚Äôt you start dispelling your study clones while we walk, and I‚Äôm going to give you your homework.‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúAw, <em> maaan</em>‚Äî‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúThis is fun homework,‚Äù Iruka interrupted, giving Naruto a stern look that shut his mouth on the complaints ready to spring forth. ‚ÄúI want you to make three, four, or at max five clones, and send them out into the village. Their job is to find a jounin or elite chuunin, and then follow them unnoticed for as long as they can. They can use any methods they can think of, but they <em> cannot </em> be caught. If they are caught, the game is over and they should dispel. Observe their habits, observe their training, and if they manage to do so unseen until nine o‚Äôclock tonight, they win and can come back to you and dispel. Any questions?‚Äù</p>
<p>‚ÄúNo,‚Äù Naruto breathed worshipfully, eyes wide with adoration. ‚ÄúIruka-sensei, that‚Äôs the best homework assignment <em> ever</em>.‚Äù</p>
<p>Iruka smiled. ‚ÄúI‚Äôm glad you think so. We‚Äôre not in a hurry; dispel your study clones first, take some time to organize their information, and then send out the homework clones when you‚Äôre ready. Now, let‚Äôs go buy you some proper ninja gear!‚Äù</p>
